OpenAI Makes ChatGPT Free for Verified U.S. Clinicians

OpenAI announced a free tier of ChatGPT for verified U.S. physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists, aiming to ease administrative burdens and support clinical decision-making.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a no-cost version of its AI assistant tailored for healthcare tasks such as documentation, medical research, and care consultation. The offering is available to verified U.S. physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists, with plans to expand to other countries over time through partnerships like the Better Evidence Network pilot.

The announcement arrives amid surging AI adoption among U.S. doctors. According to a 2026 American Medical Association survey cited in the post, 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. Millions of clinicians worldwide already use ChatGPT weekly for clinical applications, and OpenAI says clinician usage has more than doubled in the past year.

To ensure safety and accuracy, OpenAI says its physician advisors reviewed more than 700,000 model responses during development. Before launch, physicians tested 6,924 real conversations across care, documentation, and research use cases, rating 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. The company also introduced HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark using physician-authored tasks, and claims GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperforms base GPT-5.4, other models, and human physicians on that evaluation.

Why this matters

  • AI adoption among U.S. physicians has crossed a majority threshold, with 72% now using AI in clinical practice, up sharply from 48% the prior year.
  • The free offering removes a cost barrier for individual clinicians who currently rely on ChatGPT without dedicated healthcare-grade tools or compliance safeguards.
  • OpenAI's own benchmarking claims GPT-5.4 outperforms human physicians on realistic clinical tasks, suggesting a notable capability gap that could reshape clinical support tools.
