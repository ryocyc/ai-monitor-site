MiniMax Music 2.6 and M2.7 Agent Bring Self-Improvement and Cover Remixing

MiniMax has updated its Music model to version 2.6 with a Cover feature for remixing melodies, while the M2.7 Agent model adds recursive self-improvement and expanded office automation capabilities.

MiniMax Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: minimax-blog::identity::minimax-blog-minimax-music-26-bri Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

MiniMax has released Music 2.6, introducing a Cover feature that lets users remix existing melodies with deeper bass and richer instrumentation. The update covers genres including Pop, EDM, Trap, Hyperpop, Dance, Club, Sports, Video Scoring, and Epic Game styles. An API for the new version is available on the MiniMax platform.

Simultaneously, the company launched M2.7, its latest Agent model, which adds recursive self-improvement capabilities — the ability to build on its own outputs and refine them across iterations. MiniMax claims significant practical capability gains compared to the M2.5 version, particularly in engineering and coding tasks, as well as complex office workflows involving Excel, Word, and PowerPoint files with multi-round editing support.

Both releases are listed as new on MiniMax's product page, alongside refreshed versions of its Speech and Video models. MiniMax describes itself as a global leader in multi-modal AI with over 200 million users, positioning M2.7 as a step toward its broader AGI mission.

Why this matters

  • Cover feature in Music 2.6 lowers the barrier to AI-assisted music remixing, targeting creators and developers in the music production space.
  • M2.7 Agent's self-improvement loop signals a shift toward models that can iteratively refine their own outputs rather than producing one-shot results.
  • Expanded office automation in M2.7 (Excel, Word, PPT with multi-round editing) positions MiniMax directly against other agent-focused AI providers in the enterprise tooling market.
