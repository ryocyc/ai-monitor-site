Mistral March Releases: Voxtral TTS, Mistral Small 4, Leanstral, Moderation 2603

Mistral shipped four notable releases in March, spanning speech synthesis, a unified hybrid model, an open-source formal proof agent, and a refreshed moderation model.

Mistral Changelog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: mistral-changelog::identity::mistral-24.09 Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

On March 23, Mistral released Voxtral TTS (voxtral-tts-2603), a text-to-speech model featuring zero-shot voice cloning, multilingual output, and real-time streaming capabilities. The addition positions Mistral in the increasingly competitive speech synthesis market alongside providers like ElevenLabs and OpenAI's Audio API.

March 16 brought two releases in one day. First, Mistral Small 4 (mistral-small-2603) — a hybrid model that compresses instruct, reasoning, and coding tasks into a single multimodal package with a 256,000-token context window. Second, Leanstral (labs-leanstral-2603) entered the catalog as Mistral's first open-source code agent purpose-built for Lean 4 formal proof engineering, an area typically dominated by academic tooling and specialized solvers.

On March 12, Mistral Moderation 2603 (mistral-moderation-2603) launched alongside expanded Guardrails support that can now be applied at the Agent level via the guardrails parameter and on individual requests to both /v1/conversations and /v1/chat/completions.

Why this matters

  • Voxtral TTS marks Mistral's entry into voice cloning and real-time speech synthesis, an area where the provider had no prior dedicated product.
  • Mistral Small 4's hybrid instruct-reasoning-coding design in a single model with a 256k context window simplifies multi-task pipelines for developers.
  • Leanstral is an unusual addition — an open-source formal proof agent from a major commercial AI lab is relatively rare and signals Mistral's willingness to publish beyond conventional chatbot use cases.
