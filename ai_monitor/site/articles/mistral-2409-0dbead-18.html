Mistral's March releases bring Voxtral TTS, Mistral Small 4, and an open-source Lean 4 proof agent

Mistral shipped four notable releases in March, including a new text-to-speech model, a hybrid instruct-reasoning-coding model, and its first open-source formal proof agent.

Mistral Changelog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: mistral-changelog::identity::mistral-24.09 Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

On March 23, Mistral released Voxtral TTS (voxtral-tts-2603), a text-to-speech model described as state-of-the-art, with zero-shot voice cloning, multilingual support, and real-time streaming capabilities. The model joins Mistral's expanding audio portfolio alongside the earlier Voxtral Small and Voxtral Mini lines.

Just a week earlier, on March 16, the company released Mistral Small 4 (mistral-small-2603), a hybrid model that unifies instruct, reasoning, and coding tasks in a single multimodal package. The model boasts a 256k context window. On the same date, Mistral released Leanstral (labs-leanstral-2603), its first open-source code agent purpose-built for Lean 4 formal proof engineering—a relatively niche but technically demanding corner of the AI space.

March 12 saw the release of Mistral Moderation 2603 (mistral-moderation-2603), and the month also brought Custom Guardrails support configurable both at the Agent level via a `guardrails` parameter and per-request on both the `/v1/conversations` and `/v1/chat/completions` endpoints.

Why this matters

  • Voxtral TTS marks Mistral's most capable text-to-speech entry, directly competing with cloud TTS offerings from OpenAI and others.
  • Mistral Small 4's hybrid approach—combining instruct, reasoning, and coding in one model with a 256k context window—is a notable architectural step for developers needing broad capability under a single API.
  • Leanstral is Mistral's first open-source formal proof agent, positioning the company in the emerging intersection of LLMs and mathematical/software verification tooling.
