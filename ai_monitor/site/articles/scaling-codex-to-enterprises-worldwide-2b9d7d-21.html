OpenAI Launches Codex Labs to Accelerate Enterprise Adoption of Codex

OpenAI has introduced Codex Labs and a roster of seven global systems integrators to help organizations deploy its coding assistant at scale across engineering teams.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI is launching Codex Labs, a new enterprise engagement program that brings OpenAI experts directly into organizations to help them integrate Codex into real-world workflows. The company says the goal is to help enterprises move from early usage to repeatable, production-ready deployments through hands-on workshops and working sessions.

To address what OpenAI describes as demand outpacing its own capacity to help enterprises adopt quickly, the company is simultaneously partnering with seven leading global systems integrators. The confirmed partners are Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS). These firms are already using Codex internally and will help enterprise customers identify and roll out high-value use cases across the software development lifecycle, according to the announcement.

The announcement also updates adoption metrics: OpenAI says more than 4 million developers were using Codex every week as of April 21, up from 3 million reported just two weeks earlier. Enterprise customers named include Virgin Atlantic, Ramp, Notion, Cisco, and Rakuten, which are using Codex across tasks including test coverage, code review, feature building, repository reasoning, and incident response. The company notes that momentum is beginning to extend beyond engineering into broader knowledge work such as drafting, planning, and cross-tool coordination.

Why this matters

  • Seven major systems integrators are now formally enlisted to help enterprises deploy Codex, creating a structured path to scale that many organizations have been lacking.
  • OpenAI's own weekly developer count has crossed 4 million, signaling strong bottom-up adoption that enterprises are now looking to institutionalize.
  • The expansion beyond pure coding into knowledge-work tasks like drafting, planning, and incident response suggests Codex is being positioned as a broader enterprise productivity layer, not just a developer tool.
