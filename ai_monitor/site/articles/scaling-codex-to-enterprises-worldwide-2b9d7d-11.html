Scaling Codex to Enterprises Worldwide

OpenAI is pairing its new Codex Labs advisory program with a roster of major global systems integrators to accelerate enterprise deployments.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has unveiled Codex Labs, a direct advisory initiative that puts OpenAI experts inside organizations to help teams integrate Codex into real engineering and knowledge-work workflows. The program includes hands-on workshops and working sessions designed to move enterprises from early trials to repeatable, production-level deployments.

The company is simultaneously partnering with seven global systems integrators (GSIs) — Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services — to meet surging enterprise demand. These firms will identify high-value Codex use cases, manage pilot-to-production rollouts, and are themselves using Codex internally to build repeatable delivery frameworks for clients. OpenAI notes that demand from enterprises is currently outpacing its ability to serve them.

Adoption is growing rapidly on the individual side as well: more than 4 million developers now use Codex weekly, up from 3 million just two weeks prior. Early enterprise customers include Virgin Atlantic, which uses it to boost test coverage and team velocity; Ramp, for code review; Notion, for new feature development; Cisco, for reasoning across large interconnected repositories; and Rakuten, for incident response. OpenAI is also expanding Codex beyond coding to browser-based work, image generation, memory, and cross-tool knowledge tasks.

Why this matters

  • OpenAI is building a structured enterprise delivery channel rather than relying solely on self-serve adoption, acknowledging that large organizations need hands-on support to move from pilots to production.
  • The roster of seven GSIs — including Accenture, PwC, TCS, and Capgemini — signals that OpenAI is serious about global enterprise reach and is leveraging established enterprise relationships to scale.
  • Codex adoption is expanding beyond engineering into knowledge-work tasks such as drafting, planning, and cross-tool reasoning, which broadens the addressable market well beyond developer tools.
