Making ChatGPT better for clinicians

OpenAI is making ChatGPT for Clinicians available for free to verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI on April 22, 2026 launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a version of ChatGPT tailored for clinical tasks such as documentation, medical research, and care consultation. The product is now free to any individually verified U.S. clinician — physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists — with an optional HIPAA-compliant path through a Business Associate Agreement for eligible accounts.

The rollout arrives as physician AI adoption reaches a new high. According to a 2026 American Medical Association survey cited by OpenAI, 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. OpenAI also reports that clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past year. The company worked with hundreds of physician advisors to shape features including reusable skills for tasks like referral letters and prior authorizations, trusted real-time search across peer-reviewed medical sources, and automatic tracking of continuing medical education credits.

Alongside the product launch, OpenAI introduced HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks across care consult, writing and documentation, and medical research. In pre-release testing involving 6,924 conversations reviewed by physician advisors, 99.6% of responses were rated as safe and accurate. OpenAI says its GPT‑5.4 model in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperforms base GPT‑5.4, all other OpenAI and external models tested, and human physicians producing responses in their own specialty with unbounded time and web access. Expansion to clinicians outside the U.S. is planned through a pilot with the Better Evidence Network, pending local regulatory approval.

Why this matters

  • AI adoption among U.S. physicians has surged to 72%, up from 48% a year ago, signaling a watershed moment for AI in clinical settings.
  • GPT-5.4 within ChatGPT for Clinicians outperformed human physicians on standardized clinical tasks, setting a new benchmark for AI-assisted care support.
  • HIPAA support via a BAA and the release of an open professional benchmark (HealthBench Professional) introduce concrete accountability mechanisms that have been missing from most consumer AI tools in healthcare.
