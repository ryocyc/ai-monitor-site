The post examines whether the open model ecosystem has improved fast enough on efficiency and capability to surpass the gains predicted by Moore's Law — the historical doubling of transistor density roughly every two years.

The analysis draws on a series of open-weight models released through Hugging Face's model hub over that period, benchmarking them against the author's laptop hardware constraints. The practical takeaway for developers and builders is significant: local inference has become a viable alternative to cloud API calls for an expanding set of tasks, driven as much by model optimization as by hardware. The post suggests that open-source developers who once needed cloud-scale infrastructure for serious AI workloads may now find that a modern laptop — combined with efficient open weights — closes much of that gap.
