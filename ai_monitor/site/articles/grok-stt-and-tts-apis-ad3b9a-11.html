Grok Speech to Text and Text to Speech APIs

xAI has released two standalone audio APIs offering fast, accurate speech transcription and natural expressive voice synthesis at flat-rate pricing.

xAI Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: xai-blog::identity::grok-stt-and-tts-apis Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

xAI has announced two new standalone audio APIs: Grok Speech to Text (STT) and Grok Text to Speech (TTS). Built on the same stack powering Grok Voice, Tesla vehicles, and Starlink customer support, these endpoints are aimed at developers building voice agents, transcription tools, accessibility features, podcasts, and interactive audio experiences. Both APIs are available via REST and WebSocket interfaces.

The STT API supports batch transcription at $0.10 per hour and streaming at $0.20 per hour. It includes word-level timestamps, speaker diarization, multichannel separation, and Inverse Text Normalization that correctly formats numbers, dates, currencies, and other entities from raw spoken audio. A multilingual mode covers 25 or more languages with seamless switching. A benchmark table on the page shows Grok STT achieving an overall Word Error Rate of 6.9% against 9.0% for ElevenLabs, 11.0% for Deepgram, and 12.9% for AssemblyAI, across phone call, video/podcast, meeting, and telephony domains.

The TTS API is priced at $4.20 per one million characters and supports real-time speech generation via WebSocket alongside batch synthesis via REST. Developers can inject natural prosody using inline speech tags such as `[laugh]`, `[sigh]`, `[whisper]`, and SSML-style wrappers to control emphasis, pauses, and tone. Both APIs include links to the xAI developer console and documentation pages.

Why this matters

  • xAI is entering the crowded speech API market with benchmark numbers that place Grok STT ahead of well-established competitors on entity-heavy tasks like phone calls and business audio.
  • The pricing structure is simple and predictable — batch STT at $0.10/hour, streaming STT at $0.20/hour, and TTS at $4.20 per million characters — without usage tiers or hidden fees.
  • Fine-grained speech tags in the TTS API offer developers a lightweight way to add naturalness and emotion without complex markup, differentiating Grok voices from plain synthesised output.
