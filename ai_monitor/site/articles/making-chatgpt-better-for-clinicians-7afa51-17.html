OpenAI Makes ChatGPT Free for Verified Clinicians in the U.S.

OpenAI has launched a free, verified tier of ChatGPT tailored for clinical tasks, available to licensed physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI announced on April 22, 2026, that it is making ChatGPT for Clinicians available at no cost to verified individual clinicians in the U.S., including physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists. The product is designed to support documentation, medical research, and care consultations, with free access to OpenAI's current frontier models configured for healthcare use cases.

The company says clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled in the past year, and it cites a 2026 AMA survey finding that 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. ChatGPT for Clinicians includes a trusted clinical search tool drawing on peer-reviewed sources, deep research across medical journals, skills for repeatable tasks like referral letters and prior authorizations, and optional HIPAA compliance via a Business Associate Agreement. Continuing medical education credit may also be earned through evidence review conducted within the platform.

OpenAI says its models rank top among evaluated systems on Stanford's MedHELM and MedMarks benchmarks, and that before launch physician advisors reviewed more than 700,000 model responses, rating 99.6% of them as safe and accurate. The company also introduced HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks across care consult, documentation, and research, and claims GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperforms base GPT-5.4, other OpenAI and external models, and human physicians on that benchmark.

Why this matters

  • Millions of clinicians worldwide already use ChatGPT weekly, and this free verified tier significantly lowers the barrier for individual practitioners to access frontier AI models configured for clinical work.
  • The AMA survey data signals a rapid mainstreaming of AI in clinical practice, with physician adoption jumping from 48% to 72% in one year, suggesting AI is becoming a standard tool rather than an exception.
  • By publishing HealthBench Professional as an open benchmark and publishing detailed pre-release testing data, OpenAI is inviting ongoing public scrutiny of its health performance claims—a notable step toward transparency in medical AI evaluation.
