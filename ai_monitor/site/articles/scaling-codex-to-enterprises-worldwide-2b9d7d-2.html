OpenAI Scales Codex to Enterprises Worldwide with New Labs and Partner Network

OpenAI is launching Codex Labs and a network of global systems integrators to help enterprises move from individual Codex usage to full-scale, production-ready deployments.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has announced Codex Labs, a new initiative that puts OpenAI experts directly inside organizations through hands-on workshops and working sessions designed to move teams from early Codex pilots to repeatable, production-level deployment. The company simultaneously unveiled a partner network of leading global systems integrators—Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services—to handle the volume of enterprise demand it cannot meet alone. These partners are already using Codex internally and are building repeatable delivery frameworks to bring Codex capabilities to their own clients at scale.

The announcement comes as enterprise uptake is accelerating. OpenAI says more than 4 million developers were using Codex weekly as of mid-April 2026, up from 3 million just two weeks prior. Early enterprise deployments span a range of industries and use cases: Virgin Atlantic is using Codex to increase test coverage and team velocity; Ramp is using it to accelerate code review; Notion is building new product features with it; Cisco is reasoning across large, interconnected code repositories; and Rakuten is applying it to incident response. OpenAI notes that what typically begins with a single team quickly expands as leadership observes gains in speed and output.

Beyond traditional coding tasks, OpenAI is positioning Codex as a broader knowledge-work tool. The platform now supports browser-based work, image generation, memory, and ongoing task management across multiple tools and applications. Teams are using it to pull context from different systems, reason through relevant information, and produce actionable outputs like briefs, plans, and checklists. OpenAI is offering organizations a dedicated form to express interest in hosting a Codex Lab.

Why this matters

  • Seven major global systems integrators are now formally enlisted to deploy Codex at enterprise scale, giving OpenAI a distribution channel deep into large organizations worldwide.
  • Codex is expanding from a coding assistant into a broader productivity tool for non-engineering teams, potentially widening its addressable market inside each customer.
  • The rapid growth in weekly active developers—from 3 million to 4 million in two weeks—signals accelerating demand that OpenAI is racing to satisfy through both direct and partner-led channels.
