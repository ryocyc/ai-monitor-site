OpenAI Refreshes ChatGPT's Default Model with GPT-5.5 Instant

OpenAI has begun rolling out GPT-5.5 Instant, an updated version of the model powering ChatGPT by default, promising higher accuracy, fewer hallucinations, and more granular personalization controls.

Article body

OpenAI on May 5, 2026 published details of GPT-5.5 Instant, an incremental update to the model serving as ChatGPT's default. The company described the release as delivering 'smarter, clearer, and more personalized' responses compared with the prior default. Core improvements center on answer accuracy, a reduction in hallucinated outputs, and newly surfaced controls for tailoring responses to individual users.

The update is positioned as a quiet infrastructure-level refresh rather than a standalone product launch: it changes what users experience by default when opening ChatGPT without needing to select a specific model. Personalization enhancements include controls over how the model accounts for a user's history and preferences, though OpenAI has not disclosed the full technical specification or whether the update involves changes to model architecture or fine-tuning alone.

For regular ChatGPT users, the practical benefit is straightforward: fewer wrong answers and responses better tuned to personal context, without any action required on their end. For developers and teams building on OpenAI's API, the shift signals ongoing default-model quality improvements that may eventually surface in upstream API tiers.

Why this matters

  • The default model is what the majority of ChatGPT's hundreds of millions of users interact with daily, making even incremental quality gains highly consequential at scale.
  • Reduced hallucinations directly address one of the most persistent criticisms of large language models in professional and educational settings.
  • New personalization controls give end users more agency over how the model adapts to them, potentially improving utility for recurring workflows without requiring explicit prompt engineering.
