Making ChatGPT better for clinicians

OpenAI is offering free ChatGPT access to verified U.S. physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists, alongside a new clinical benchmark called HealthBench Professional.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI on April 22, 2026 launched ChatGPT for Clinicians — a version of ChatGPT tailored for clinical tasks including documentation, medical research, and care consultation. Verified U.S. physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists can now access it for free, with optional HIPAA compliance via a Business Associate Agreement for eligible accounts. The product includes skills for repeatable workflows such as referral letters and prior authorizations, trusted cited search across peer-reviewed medical sources, and a deep research mode for reviewing medical literature.

The announcement arrives as AI adoption among U.S. physicians has surged. According to an American Medical Association survey cited by OpenAI, 72% of physicians now report using AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. ChatGPT usage among clinicians worldwide has more than doubled over the past year. OpenAI says its physician advisors have reviewed over 700,000 model responses to date, and pre-release testing involving 6,924 conversations in real clinical settings found that physicians rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate.

The release also introduces HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for clinician chat tasks covering care consultation, writing and documentation, and medical research. OpenAI states that GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperforms base GPT-5.4, other OpenAI and external models, and responses produced by human physicians working with unbounded time and web access. A Health Blueprint document outlining recommendations for responsible AI integration in U.S. healthcare was published alongside the launch.

Why this matters

  • Free access removes a cost barrier for individual clinicians who have been using consumer ChatGPT without dedicated clinical tools or compliance safeguards.
  • The 24-percentage-point jump in physician AI adoption in one year underscores how rapidly clinical workflows are shifting toward AI-assisted care.
  • HealthBench Professional's open benchmark — with physician-authored tasks, multi-stage adjudication, and deliberate adversarial testing — could set a new standard for evaluating AI in healthcare settings.
