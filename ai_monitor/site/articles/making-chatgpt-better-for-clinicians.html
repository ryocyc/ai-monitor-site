OpenAI Makes ChatGPT Free for Verified U.S. Clinicians, Publishes Health Benchmark

OpenAI has released a dedicated, free version of ChatGPT for verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States, alongside a new open benchmark that puts GPT-5.4 ahead of human physicians...

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

ChatGPT for Clinicians is a purpose-built variant of the general ChatGPT product, tailored to documentation, care consultation, and medical research workflows. Starting today, any verified U.S. physician, NP, PA, or pharmacist can access it at no cost. The product includes frontier-model access for clinical questions, reusable "skills" for repeatable tasks such as prior authorization letters and patient instructions, real-time cited search across peer-reviewed medical literature, and the ability to delegate journal reviews to the model. Eligible clinicians can also earn continuing medical education (CME) credits directly from evidence reviews conducted inside ChatGPT, without separate coursework.

The launch is backed by HealthBench Professional, a new open benchmark designed specifically for clinician-facing chat tasks. According to OpenAI, physician advisors reviewed more than 700,000 model responses during development and ran 6,924 pre-release conversations across real clinical care, documentation, and research settings; 99.6% of those responses were rated safe and accurate. On a curated subset of 355 examples where three independent physicians provided ground-truth citations, ChatGPT for Clinicians cited those sources more often than the physicians themselves. OpenAI reports that GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperforms base GPT-5.4, all other OpenAI and external models, and human physicians on the benchmark. About a third of benchmark examples involved deliberate red-teaming by physicians, and the selected conversations were chosen to be 3.5 times more difficult than average.

The company also released a Health Blueprint outlining recommendations for responsible AI integration in U.S. healthcare settings, and made optional HIPAA compliance available through a Business Associate Agreement for accounts that handle protected health information. International expansion is planned through a pilot with the Better Evidence Network, beginning in the coming months as local regulations permit.

Why this matters

  • Clinician adoption of AI has crossed a threshold: 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% a year ago, according to an AMA survey cited in the announcement.
  • The benchmark results claim GPT-5.4 outperforms human physicians on clinical citation accuracy, a competitive yardstick that could shape how hospitals evaluate AI tools for patient-facing use.
  • Free access removes a financial barrier that has historically limited individual clinicians—outside large health systems with enterprise contracts—from using frontier AI in daily practice.
