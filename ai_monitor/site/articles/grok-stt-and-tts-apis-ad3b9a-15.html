xAI Launches Grok Speech-to-Text and Text-to-Speech APIs

xAI has released two standalone audio APIs — Grok Speech to Text and Grok Text to Speech — built on the same technology stack that powers Grok Voice, Tesla vehicles, and Starlink customer support.

xAI Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: xai-blog::identity::grok-stt-and-tts-apis Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

The Grok Speech to Text API handles both batch transcription and real-time streaming via REST and WebSocket endpoints. It includes word-level timestamps, speaker diarization, multichannel support, and Inverse Text Normalization that converts spoken numbers, dates, and currencies into structured output. The service covers more than 25 languages, switching between them mid-session without interruption.

Pricing is straightforward: $0.10 per hour for batch transcription and $0.20 per hour for streaming. xAI also benchmarks its STT model against ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and AssemblyAI across phone calls, meetings, video, and telephony, claiming an overall word error rate of 6.9% versus competitors ranging from 9.0% to 12.9%.

The Grok Text to Speech API generates natural, expressive voice output from long-form text using a REST or WebSocket connection. Developers can add inline speech tags — such as [laugh], [sigh], and [whisper] — to control prosody and emotion without complex markup. TTS is priced at $4.20 per one million characters. Both APIs are available now through the xAI console.

Why this matters

  • xAI is entering a crowded speech-API market with competitive pricing and benchmark claims that outperform established players on entity-heavy transcription tasks.
  • The TTS API's inline speech-tag system offers developers a simpler alternative to conventional SSML for controlling voice nuance.
  • Availability alongside existing Grok Voice integrations (Tesla, Starlink) suggests the APIs are production-hardened before general release.
