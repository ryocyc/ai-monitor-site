Grok Speech to Text and Text to Speech APIs

xAI has released two standalone audio APIs—Grok Speech to Text and Grok Text to Speech—built on the same technology powering Grok Voice in Tesla vehicles and Starlink customer support.

xAI Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: xai-blog::identity::grok-stt-and-tts-apis Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

The APIs give developers two independent endpoints for integrating speech capabilities into applications. Grok STT supports both batch and real-time transcription via REST and WebSocket, and includes features like word-level timestamps, speaker diarization, and multichannel audio handling. The TTS endpoint offers fine-grained prosody control through simple inline tags such as [laugh], [sigh], and [whisper], with support for both REST and WebSocket streaming.

Pricing is straightforward and usage-based. Speech to Text costs $0.10 per hour for batch processing and $0.20 per hour for streaming. Text to Speech is priced at $4.20 per one million characters. xAI benchmarks its STT model against ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and AssemblyAI across domains including phone calls, meetings, video and podcasts, and telephony. In most categories, Grok STT posted lower word error rates than the comparison models, with an overall WER of 6.9% versus 9–12.9% for competitors.

The STT API covers more than 25 languages with seamless language switching. The TTS system is the same voice technology embedded in Grok Voice, Tesla vehicles, and Starlink customer support, and xAI offers a live voice playground for testing before integration.

Why this matters

  • xAI is competing directly with established speech API providers—Deepgram, AssemblyAI, and ElevenLabs—using benchmark data that shows lower word error rates across most evaluated domains.
  • Pricing is transparent and predictable, with no subscription minimums, which could lower the barrier for developers building voice-enabled products at scale.
  • Tying the API to technology already in production at Tesla and Starlink signals enterprise-grade reliability and real-world validation beyond typical benchmark testing.
