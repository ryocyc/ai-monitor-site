Making ChatGPT better for clinicians

OpenAI is making a clinical-purpose version of ChatGPT free to verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a version of its AI assistant tailored for documentation, medical research, and care consultation. The product is available at no cost to verified U.S. clinicians — physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists — with optional HIPAA support through a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) for eligible accounts. The company says the rollout is designed to reduce administrative burden and help clinicians focus on patient care.

The announcement cites a 2026 American Medical Association survey reporting that 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. ChatGPT usage among clinicians has more than doubled over the past year, according to OpenAI, which frames the free offering as a next step in supporting healthcare workflows. Features include advanced frontier models for healthcare use, reusable "skills" for tasks like referral letters and prior authorization, real-time search across peer-reviewed medical sources, and a deep research function that compiles literature reviews. Clinicians can also earn continuing medical education (CME) credits by conducting evidence reviews within ChatGPT.

The release also introduces HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for clinician chat tasks covering care consult, documentation, and research. According to OpenAI, physician advisors reviewed over 700,000 model responses before launch and rated 99.6% as safe and accurate. On the benchmark, OpenAI's GPT‑5.4 outperformed human physicians producing responses in their own specialty with unbounded time and web access. OpenAI is also publishing a Health Blueprint with recommendations for responsible AI integration in U.S. healthcare.

Why this matters

  • Free access could significantly expand AI adoption among individual clinicians, not just health systems with enterprise contracts.
  • The optional HIPAA compliance pathway (BAA) makes ChatGPT for Clinicians more viable for handling sensitive patient information.
  • The HealthBench Professional benchmark offers a public, physician-authored standard for evaluating AI performance in real clinical chat scenarios.
