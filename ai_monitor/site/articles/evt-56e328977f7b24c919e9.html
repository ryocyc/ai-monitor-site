llama.cpp Lands Hexagon HMX Clock Fix for v75+ Platforms in Release b8934

The ggml-org/llama.cpp project shipped release b8934 on April 26, patching a clock-request guard issue for Qualcomm Hexagon/HMX silicon on v75+ hardware, alongside refreshed CUDA 13.1 binaries and KleidiAI-enabled Apple Silicon builds.

Article body

Release b8934 of llama.cpp, the widely used open-source inference runtime for large language models, landed on April 26 with a targeted fix for Qualcomm Hexagon/HMX processors: a guard for HMX clock requests on v75+ platforms (pull #22377). The change addresses a condition that could arise during inference on newer Hexagon-based hardware running llama.cpp's tensor-processing pipeline.

The release ships prebuilt binaries across a broad set of targets, including refreshed Windows CUDA 12 and CUDA 13.1 (13.1 DLLs) packages, macOS Apple Silicon builds with and without KleidiAI acceleration, iOS XCFramework archives, Linux builds for CPU, Vulkan, ROCm 7.2, OpenVINO, and SYCL (FP32 and FP16), Android arm64, and new openEuler builds covering both x86 (310p, 910b with ACL Graph) and aarch64 (310p) configurations.

For inference engineers and teams deploying quantized LLMs on custom or embedded silicon, the Hexagon HMX fix resolves a real barrier on v75+ hardware that would previously have caused instability. The expanded CUDA 13.1 and KleidiAI support signals continued attention to performance-critical backends on Apple and NVIDIA GPUs respectively, while the openEuler additions broaden deployment options on Chinese enterprise Linux distributions.

Why this matters

  • The Hexagon HMX v75+ clock guard fixes a potential crash or instability path for anyone running quantized LLM inference on Qualcomm AI silicon — a segment growing in edge and mobile deployment scenarios.
  • Prebuilt CUDA 13.1 DLLs and KleidiAI-enabled macOS binaries reduce setup friction for developers targeting NVIDIA RTX/H100 and Apple M-series chips without compiling from source.
  • openEuler support — including 910b with ACL Graph — extends llama.cpp into enterprise and government Linux environments in China, an increasingly relevant deployment target.
