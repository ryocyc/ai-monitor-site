OpenAI Launches Codex Transformation Partners to Scale AI Adoption Across Enterprises

OpenAI has unveiled a new enterprise partner program that pairs large organizations with global systems integrators to deploy Codex beyond pilot stages into full production workflows.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has announced Codex Transformation Partners, an enterprise program designed to accelerate adoption of its Codex AI coding tool across industries. The initiative pairs organizations with global systems integrators—including Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services—to move Codex from early pilots into repeatable, production-ready deployments. The company says demand for guided enterprise adoption has outpaced its internal capacity to keep pace.

The announcement arrives alongside fresh adoption figures: OpenAI reports that weekly developers using Codex grew from more than 3 million in early April to over 4 million just two weeks later. Early enterprise deployments span a range of use cases. Virgin Atlantic is using Codex to expand test coverage and reduce technical debt. Ramp is applying it to code review. Notion is using it to build new product features quickly, while Cisco is leveraging it to navigate large, interconnected codebases, and Rakuten is using it for incident response. OpenAI notes that usage often starts with a single team before expanding as leadership observes productivity gains.

Codex is also broadening beyond pure coding into browser-based work, image generation, and memory-assisted tasks across tools and applications. OpenAI says it is beginning to reach knowledge-work functions beyond engineering, helping teams pull together context from multiple sources, draft briefs, and manage follow-ups. To support those broader ambitions, Codex Labs—OpenAI's direct consulting arm—will also run hands-on workshops inside organizations to map Codex to existing workflows.

Why this matters

  • Enterprise AI adoption has long been slowed by integration complexity and organizational resistance; a structured partner ecosystem removes some of that friction by bringing experienced integrators into the deployment process.
  • The rapid growth from 3 million to 4 million weekly Codex developers in just two weeks signals accelerating demand that the company's own teams alone cannot satisfy, making the GSI network a structural necessity rather than a marketing add-on.
  • Codex's expansion beyond coding into browser, image, and memory tasks suggests OpenAI is positioning it as a broader knowledge-work assistant, not just a developer tool—widening its addressable market inside enterprise accounts.
