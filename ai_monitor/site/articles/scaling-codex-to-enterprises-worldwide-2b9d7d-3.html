Scaling Codex to enterprises worldwide

OpenAI is launching Codex Labs and a global systems integrator partner network to help enterprises move from individual developer use to organization-wide Codex deployment.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched Codex Labs, a direct-engagement program that puts OpenAI experts inside organizations to help teams integrate Codex into real workflows. The company is also partnering with seven major global systems integrators—Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services—to scale enterprise adoption more broadly. According to the post, demand from enterprises is outpacing OpenAI's ability to support adoption at the pace companies want, making the GSI channel a key part of the rollout strategy.

The post cites several named enterprise customers already using Codex in production. Virgin Atlantic is using it to increase test coverage and team velocity while reducing technical debt. Ramp is applying it to code review, Notion to feature development, Cisco to reason across large interconnected code repositories, and Rakuten to incident response. The post notes that adoption is growing quickly—Codex went from 3 million weekly active developers in early April to more than 4 million just two weeks later.

OpenAI is also positioning Codex as expanding beyond pure software engineering. The company points to capabilities in browser-based work, image generation, memory, and cross-tool orchestration, enabling tasks like drafting briefs, plans, and follow-ups across different applications. The stated goal is to help not just engineering teams but every function within an enterprise move faster.

Why this matters

  • OpenAI is explicitly building a services channel—GSIs included—to bridge the gap between individual developer sign-ups and organization-wide deployment, a pattern typically seen in enterprise software rather than AI APIs.
  • Codex is being pushed beyond coding into broader knowledge-work tasks like document drafting and cross-tool reasoning, signaling OpenAI's intent to position it as a general enterprise productivity layer.
  • Seven named GSIs—Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and TCS—signals a coordinated go-to-market push that could rapidly expand Codex's enterprise footprint globally.
