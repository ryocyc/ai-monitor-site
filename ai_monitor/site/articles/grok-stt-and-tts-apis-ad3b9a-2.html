Grok Speech to Text and Text to Speech APIs

xAI has released two standalone audio APIs — Grok Speech to Text and Grok Text to Speech — promising high accuracy, natural expressive voices, and straightforward pricing.

xAI Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: xai-blog::identity::grok-stt-and-tts-apis Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

xAI announced two new standalone audio APIs on April 17, 2026: Grok Speech to Text (STT) and Grok Text to Speech (TTS). Built on the same infrastructure that powers Grok Voice in Tesla vehicles and Starlink customer support, these endpoints let developers integrate speech features — including voice agents, real-time transcription tools, and interactive audio experiences — via REST or WebSocket APIs.

Grok STT offers batch transcription at $0.10 per hour and streaming at $0.20 per hour, with features such as word-level timestamps, speaker diarization, and multichannel audio support. Its Inverse Text Normalization feature converts raw spoken output into structured text, correctly handling numbers, dates, currencies, and other entities. The company benchmarks the model against ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and AssemblyAI, reporting a 5.0% word error rate on phone call entity tasks versus 12–21% for competitors. Grok TTS, priced at $4.20 per million characters, adds fine-grained prosody control through inline speech tags like `[laugh]`, `[sigh]`, and `[whisper]`.

Both APIs support 25 or more languages with what xAI describes as seamless language switching. Grok TTS includes an online voice playground, while STT documentation and API console links are available directly from the announcement. xAI positions these as developer-friendly tools targeting business use cases including medical, legal, and financial transcription.

Why this matters

  • Grok STT posts lower reported word error rates than leading rivals on benchmark domains such as phone calls and meetings, suggesting competitive quality for enterprise transcription workloads.
  • TTS pricing at $4.20 per million characters is a transparent, flat rate — a contrast to tiered or per-request models common in the market — potentially simplifying cost forecasting for developers.
  • The addition of speech tags for prosody and emotion in TTS, plus speaker diarization in STT, goes beyond basic transcription or voice synthesis, targeting richer real-time audio applications.
