OpenAI Scales Codex to Enterprises Worldwide with New Partner Network

OpenAI has expanded its Codex AI coding assistant to over 4 million weekly developers and launched a new enterprise partner program to accelerate adoption across large organizations.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI's Codex AI coding assistant is now used by more than 4 million developers weekly, up from 3 million just two weeks prior, signaling rapid uptake among both individual programmers and enterprise teams. Major companies including Virgin Atlantic, Ramp, Notion, Cisco, and Rakuten have integrated Codex into real engineering workflows, applying it to tasks ranging from test coverage and code review to incident response and large repository analysis.

To support enterprise-scale deployment, OpenAI is partnering with seven global systems integrators—Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS)—to help large organizations move from pilot programs to production-ready implementations. These partners are also using Codex internally to reshape their own development practices before offering it to clients.

Codex is simultaneously expanding beyond pure coding into broader knowledge-work tasks such as browser-based work, image generation, memory capabilities, and cross-tool automation, positioning it as a productivity layer for entire organizations rather than just engineering teams.

Why this matters

  • The 33% jump in weekly developers in two weeks shows Codex adoption is accelerating sharply, with enterprises now driving the next wave of growth.
  • Seven major systems integrators are actively training on and deploying Codex, meaning it could become a standard enterprise tool within the next 12–18 months.
  • OpenAI's push to extend Codex beyond coding into general knowledge work signals a broader ambition to embed AI assistance across all business functions, not just software teams.
