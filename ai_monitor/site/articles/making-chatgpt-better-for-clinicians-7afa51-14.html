OpenAI Offers Free ChatGPT to Verified Healthcare Professionals

OpenAI has released ChatGPT for Clinicians at no cost to verified U.S. physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists, with a new benchmark claiming GPT‑5.4 outperforms human doctors on clinical tasks.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a version of ChatGPT tailored for clinical tasks such as documentation, medical research, and care consultations, and is offering it free of charge to any verified U.S. clinician — including physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists. The product builds on the earlier ChatGPT for Healthcare offering but is aimed at individual clinicians rather than health systems, giving them direct access without an organizational contract.

The company cites a 2026 American Medical Association survey reporting that 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. OpenAI says clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past year, and it frames free access as the next step in its mission to ensure AGI benefits all of humanity. The product includes advanced frontier models, reusable skills for repeatable workflows such as referral letters and prior authorization, cited search across peer‑reviewed medical sources, and automated CME credit tracking for evidence review performed in ChatGPT. Optional HIPAA compliance via a Business Associate Agreement is available for eligible accounts, and conversations are not used to train models.

Alongside the release, OpenAI is publishing HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks across care consult, writing and documentation, and medical research. In pre‑release testing involving 6,924 conversations reviewed by physician advisors, GPT‑5.4 within ChatGPT for Clinicians outperformed base GPT‑5.4, all other OpenAI and external models tested, and human physicians on the benchmark. Physicians rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. OpenAI says access will expand internationally over time, beginning with a pilot through the Better Evidence Network.

Why this matters

  • Free access for verified individual clinicians removes the cost barrier that previously limited AI tools to large health systems with compliance infrastructure.
  • The claimed outperformance of GPT‑5.4 over human physicians on a physician‑adjudicated benchmark is a notable claim that, if reproducible, represents a milestone in clinical AI evaluation.
  • The 72% physician AI adoption rate reported by the AMA signals that AI integration in clinical practice is no longer a fringe behavior but a mainstream trend, creating urgency around safety and accuracy standards.
