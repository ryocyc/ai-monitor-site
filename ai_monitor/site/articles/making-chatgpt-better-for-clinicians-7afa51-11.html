OpenAI Launches Free ChatGPT for Clinicians in the U.S.

OpenAI is offering free ChatGPT access to verified physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists in the United States, expanding AI support for clinical documentation, medical research, and care consultation.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a version of ChatGPT tailored for medical professionals and available at no cost to verified individual clinicians in the U.S. The product targets physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists, giving them access to frontier AI models built for healthcare use cases — including support for documentation, care consultation, and medical literature review.

The offering includes tools such as reusable skills for repeatable clinical workflows (referral letters, prior authorizations, patient instructions), real-time cited answers drawn from peer-reviewed medical sources, and deep research across medical journals. Clinicians can also earn continuing medical education credits by conducting evidence reviews through the platform. HIPAA support is optional, available through a Business Associate Agreement for eligible accounts, and conversations are not used to train models.

The announcement comes as AI adoption among U.S. physicians has surged: an American Medical Association survey cited by OpenAI found that 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. OpenAI says clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past year. The company also introduced HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for clinician chat tasks, and claims its GPT-5.4 model outperforms human physicians on that benchmark. Global expansion is planned, with a pilot via the Better Evidence Network outside the U.S. in the coming months.

Why this matters

  • Free access for verified U.S. clinicians could meaningfully lower the barrier to AI-assisted clinical workflows at a moment when physician burnout and administrative burden are acute.
  • OpenAI reports its GPT-5.4 model outperforms human physicians on the newly released HealthBench Professional benchmark, offering a concrete (if self-reported) performance benchmark for clinical AI.
  • The surge in physician AI adoption — 72% now, up from 48% — signals a rapid shift in how clinicians integrate AI tools, making the availability of a dedicated clinical version timely.
