xAI Launches Grok Speech-to-Text and Text-to-Speech APIs

xAI has released two standalone audio APIs — Grok Speech to Text and Grok Text to Speech — promising high accuracy, low latency, and natural expressive voices across more than 25 languages.

xAI Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: xai-blog::identity::grok-stt-and-tts-apis Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

xAI announced on April 17, 2026, two new standalone audio APIs: Grok Speech to Text and Grok Text to Speech. The APIs are built on the same underlying stack used by Grok Voice, Tesla vehicles, and Starlink customer support, and are designed to let developers integrate speech capabilities into voice agents, transcription tools, accessibility products, and interactive audio experiences without relying on Grok's chat interface. Grok STT offers both a REST API for batch transcription and a WebSocket API for real-time streaming, with features including word-level timestamps, speaker diarization, multichannel support, and Inverse Text Normalization that converts raw spoken numbers, dates, and currencies into structured output. Grok TTS similarly provides REST and WebSocket endpoints and introduces fine-grained control through inline speech tags such as `[laugh]`, `[sigh]`, and `[whisper]` to adjust prosody and emotion without complex markup.

Pricing is straightforward and transparent: STT is billed at $0.10 per hour for batch processing and $0.20 per hour for streaming, while TTS is priced at $4.20 per one million characters. xAI has published benchmark comparisons showing Grok STT outperforming ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and AssemblyAI across phone call entities (5.0% word error rate vs. the next best at 12.0%), video and podcast transcription (2.4%), meeting transcription (10.9%), and telephone tasks (9.3%), with an overall word error rate of 6.9%. The system also claims to handle more than 25 languages, allowing seamless in-call language switching without interrupting transcription. The company points to business-critical domains — medical, legal, and financial transcription — as particular strengths, noting strong entity recognition on phone calls and meetings.

Both APIs are accessible via the xAI API console, with documentation for Speech to Text and Text to Speech available in the xAI developer docs. A voice playground lets developers sample TTS output before writing code, and the company notes that Grok STT goes beyond raw word output when formatting is enabled, applying advanced Inverse Text Normalization to produce cleaner structured results. The launch positions xAI as a direct competitor in the increasingly crowded speech-as-a-service market, competing not only on price but on accuracy benchmarks and tight integration with the broader xAI model ecosystem.

Why this matters

  • xAI is directly comparing Grok STT against established players (ElevenLabs, Deepgram, AssemblyAI) using publicly reported Word Error Rate benchmarks, which is rare and worth scrutiny.
  • The $0.10–$0.20 per hour pricing for STT and $4.20 per million characters for TTS is straightforward and public, making cost estimation easier than competitors with opaque enterprise pricing.
  • Features like speaker diarization, word-level timestamps, and speech tags for emotional prosody target professional voice use cases beyond basic transcription, potentially broadening xAI's developer audience.
