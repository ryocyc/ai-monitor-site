Scaling Codex to enterprises worldwide

OpenAI is pairing its new Codex Labs onboarding service with a roster of global systems integrators to accelerate enterprise adoption of its coding assistant.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched Codex Labs, a structured enterprise onboarding program that sends OpenAI experts directly into organizations to run hands-on workshops and integration sessions. The goal is to move teams from early Codex experiments to repeatable, production-grade deployments in areas like code review, test coverage, and repository reasoning. Interest from enterprises, the company says, is outpacing its ability to serve them quickly enough on its own.

To close that gap, OpenAI has also partnered with seven global systems integrators (GSIs) — Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services — to carry Codex deployment work at scale. These firms are using Codex internally as they build repeatable service offerings for their own enterprise clients. A quote from Accenture's Chief AI Officer highlights the speed shift: professionals are moving from static requirements to working solutions in hours rather than weeks.

The announcement arrives two weeks after OpenAI reported that weekly active Codex developers had grown from three million to more than four million. Early enterprise adopters include Virgin Atlantic (boosting test coverage and team velocity), Ramp (accelerating code review), Notion (rapidly building new features), Cisco (reasoning across large repositories), and Rakuten (incident response). Beyond pure coding, OpenAI notes that knowledge-worker teams are beginning to use Codex for tasks like composing briefs, plans, and follow-ups across a mix of tools.

Why this matters

  • OpenAI has enlisted seven major GSIs — Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and TCS — to shoulder enterprise Codex rollouts, effectively outsourcing implementation capacity to firms that already operate inside large organizations.
  • The weekly developer count for Codex crossed four million in roughly two weeks, indicating rapid individual adoption that is now being followed by formal organizational push.
  • Enterprise use cases are expanding from pure coding into knowledge work — briefs, plans, and cross-tool reasoning — suggesting OpenAI sees a broader play beyond engineering teams.
