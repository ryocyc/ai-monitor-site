Grok Speech to Text and Text to Speech APIs Launch

xAI has released two standalone audio APIs offering speech transcription and synthesis with multilingual coverage, speaker diarization, and a comparison against leading competitors.

xAI Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: xai-blog::identity::grok-stt-and-tts-apis Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

xAI has launched Grok Speech to Text and Grok Text to Speech as standalone APIs, giving developers direct access to the audio capabilities that already power Grok Voice in Tesla vehicles and Starlink customer support. Both endpoints are accessible via REST and WebSocket, covering both batch processing and real-time streaming use cases.

Grok STT supports 25+ languages with features including word-level timestamps, speaker diarization, and multichannel audio separation. An advanced Inverse Text Normalization step converts spoken numbers, dates, and currencies into clean structured output. In benchmarks published on the announcement page, Grok STT achieved an overall Word Error Rate of 6.9% against 9.0% for ElevenLabs, 11.0% for Deepgram, and 12.9% for AssemblyAI, with particularly strong performance on phone call entities at 5.0% versus 21.3% for AssemblyAI.

Grok TTS generates speech from text with inline speech tags such as `[laugh]`, `[sigh]`, and `[whisper]` for expressive control. Pricing is straightforward: STT is $0.10 per hour for batch and $0.20 per hour for streaming, while TTS is $4.20 per one million characters, with no hidden fees. Documentation and a voice playground are available through the xAI API console.

Why this matters

  • Grok STT benchmarks show lower word error rates than ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and AssemblyAI across phone, video, meetings, and telephony domains.
  • Both APIs are offered as standalone endpoints, meaning developers can integrate speech capabilities into any application without requiring Grok's broader chat interface.
  • The pricing model is flat and usage-based with no hidden fees, and TTS pricing is billed per million characters rather than per audio minute.
