OpenAI Makes ChatGPT Free for Verified U.S. Clinicians

OpenAI announced on April 22, 2026, that it is offering free ChatGPT access to verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States, as demand for AI in clinical settings continues to grow.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI is rolling out ChatGPT for Clinicians, a version of its AI assistant tailored for tasks like clinical documentation, medical research, and care consultation. Starting immediately, any verified U.S. physician, NP, PA, or pharmacist can access the tool at no cost. The company cited an American Medical Association survey finding that 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year, and noted that clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past year.

The offering includes access to OpenAI's frontier models for healthcare, a trusted medical search function drawing on peer-reviewed sources, deep research across medical journals, and skills for repeatable workflows such as referral letters, prior authorizations, and patient instructions. Clinicians can also earn continuing medical education (CME) credits through evidence review conducted within ChatGPT. An optional HIPAA-compliant mode is available through a Business Associate Agreement for accounts that handle protected health information.

Alongside the product launch, OpenAI introduced HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks covering care consult, writing and documentation, and medical research. The benchmark used 6,924 conversations tested by physician advisors in their daily clinical work. Across those conversations, physicians rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. OpenAI's GPT-5.4 model outperformed base GPT-5.4, all other OpenAI and external models tested, and human physicians on the benchmark.

Why this matters

  • AI adoption among U.S. physicians has surged to 72%, according to an AMA survey, signaling a rapid shift in how clinicians integrate AI into daily practice.
  • The free offering lowers the barrier to entry for individual clinicians, not just large health systems, potentially expanding AI-assisted care across a wider range of clinical settings.
  • OpenAI's internal benchmark claims GPT-5.4 outperforms human physicians on real clinician tasks — a notable claim that, if independently verified, would mark a milestone for AI in healthcare.
