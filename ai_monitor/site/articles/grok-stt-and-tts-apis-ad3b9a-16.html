xAI Launches Grok Speech to Text and Text to Speech APIs

xAI has released two standalone audio APIs — Grok Speech to Text and Grok Text to Speech — promising high accuracy, natural-sounding voices, and multilingual support built on the same infrastructure powering Grok Voice in Tesla vehicles...

xAI Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: xai-blog::identity::grok-stt-and-tts-apis Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

Grok Speech to Text is offered at $0.10 per hour for batch processing and $0.20 per hour for real-time streaming via WebSocket. The API includes word-level timestamps, speaker diarization to track different speakers, multichannel audio support, and Inverse Text Normalization that converts spoken numbers, dates, and currencies into structured text. xAI's benchmarks put Grok STT's overall word error rate at 6.9%, comparing favorably against ElevenLabs (9.0%), Deepgram (11.0%), and AssemblyAI (12.9%), with the largest gap on phone call entity recognition at 5.0% versus 21.3% for AssemblyAI.

Grok Text to Speech is priced at $4.20 per one million characters and supports fine-grained control through inline speech tags such as [laugh], [sigh], [whisper], and various prosody markers, letting developers add natural prosody and emotional nuance to generated audio without complex markup. Both APIs are accessible via REST endpoints for batch work and WebSocket for streaming use cases. The STT model covers more than 25 languages with seamless switching, according to xAI.

The announcement positions Grok Voice — the same technology now packaged into standalone APIs — as the system already deployed in Tesla vehicles and Starlink's customer support operations. xAI is directing developers to its API console for rate limits and full pricing details, with separate documentation pages for each API.

Why this matters

  • xAI's STT benchmarks show a meaningful accuracy gap on business-critical domains like phone call entities, where Grok's 5.0% word error rate is well below competing services.
  • The flat per-hour and per-character pricing model for STT and TTS respectively offers developers predictable costs at a time when speech API pricing varies widely.
  • Embedding Grok Voice — already live in Tesla vehicles and Starlink support — into developer-accessible APIs extends that infrastructure into third-party voice agents, transcription tools, and accessibility products.
