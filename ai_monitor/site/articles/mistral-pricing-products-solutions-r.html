Mistral AI Pricing Plans

Mistral AI has published tiered pricing for its Le Chat assistant, ranging from a free tier to a $24.99-per-user Team plan, with an Enterprise option for custom deployments.

Mistral Pricing 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: mistral-pricing::identity::mistral-pricing-products-solutions-r Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

Mistral AI's pricing page shows four distinct tiers: a Free plan built around the Le Chat personal assistant, a Pro plan at $14.99 per month, a Team plan at $24.99 per month per user, and a custom Enterprise tier. The free Le Chat tier offers access to Mistral's state-of-the-art AI models, up to 500 saved memories, image generation, project-based chat organization, and a directory of more than 40 enterprise connectors.

The Pro plan targets users who need higher productivity, bundling Mistral Vibe for all-day coding, increased message and web search limits, extended thinking and deep research reports, up to 15 GB of document storage, and up to 1,000 projects. The Team plan, priced at $24.99 per user per month, adds collaborative workspace features for teams, including up to 30 GB of storage per user, domain name verification, data export, and audit logs, among other administrative controls.

Enterprise customers receive private deployments powered by custom models, custom UI, and tools, with pricing handled through direct contact. The comparison table breaks down per-tier allowances across messaging, thinking modes, deep research, memories, document uploads, verified news, connector access, image generation, and code interpreter usage — with the Free tier anchoring most limits and higher tiers unlocking multiplicative boosts on a per-plan basis.

Why this matters

  • The Free plan's inclusion of 40+ enterprise connectors makes Mistral's Le Chat a notably accessible entry point for individuals wanting to experiment with AI-driven business integrations.
  • The introduction of a paid Team tier signals Mistral's push beyond individual users toward organizations, offering storage and administrative features that the Free and Pro plans lack.
  • The feature comparison table reveals a structured approach to scaling limits — Free caps most capabilities while Pro and Team multiply those caps — giving users a clear picture of what each tier unlocks before needing an Enterprise contract.
