ChatGPT for Clinicians: Free Access for Verified US Healthcare Professionals

OpenAI has launched a free version of ChatGPT built for clinical work, offering U.S. physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists access to frontier AI models for documentation, medical research, and care consul...

Article body

ChatGPT for Clinicians is now available at no cost to verified individual healthcare professionals in the United States, OpenAI announced on April 22, 2026. The product targets common clinical tasks including documentation, medical research, care consultations, and administrative workflows like referral letters and prior authorizations. Access requires identity verification and covers physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists.

The launch reflects a sharp rise in AI adoption among U.S. clinicians. An American Medical Association survey cited by OpenAI found 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. Clinician usage of ChatGPT specifically has more than doubled over the past year. The company says millions of clinicians globally use ChatGPT weekly in clinical settings.

OpenAI says its models ranked top-performing on third-party healthcare evaluations including Stanford's MedHELM and MedMarks, and that its physician advisor team has reviewed more than 700,000 model responses. In pre-release testing, physicians rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate across 6,924 conversations. The company is also releasing HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for clinician chat tasks, and plans to expand access outside the United States in collaboration with the Better Evidence Network.

Why this matters

  • Free access removes a cost barrier for individual clinicians seeking AI support in patient care and administrative tasks.
  • A 72% physician AI adoption rate, up sharply from 48% last year, signals that AI is becoming routine in clinical workflows rather than experimental.
  • The release of HealthBench Professional as an open benchmark gives the healthcare and AI communities a shared tool to measure and track model performance on real clinical tasks.
