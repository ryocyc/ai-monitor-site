OpenAI Makes ChatGPT for Clinicians Free for Verified U.S. Healthcare Professionals

OpenAI is offering its clinically-focused version of ChatGPT at no cost to verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI on April 22, 2026 launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a version of its chatbot built for medical documentation, care consultation, and research. The product is free for any individually verified U.S. clinician——physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists——and includes access to frontier AI models, reusable skills for repeatable workflows like prior authorizations and referral letters, and a trusted clinical search tool drawing on peer-reviewed medical literature. Optional HIPAA compliance via a Business Associate Agreement is also available for eligible accounts.

The announcement arrives alongside data OpenAI says reflects surging clinician adoption: a 2026 American Medical Association survey found 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year, and ChatGPT usage among clinicians has more than doubled over the same period. OpenAI says its physician advisors have reviewed more than 700,000 model responses to date, with a new response evaluated every few minutes. On third-party benchmarks including Stanford’s MedHELM and MedMarks, OpenAI’s models ranked as the top-performing systems for real-world healthcare use.

The launch also introduces HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks across care consultation, documentation, and research. In pre-release testing, 6,924 conversations were evaluated by physicians in their daily clinical work, with 99.6% of responses rated as safe and accurate. OpenAI says GPT-5.4, powering ChatGPT for Clinicians, outperformed base GPT-5.4, other OpenAI and external models, and human physicians on the benchmark——though the company notes that ChatGPT for Clinicians is designed to support clinicians, not replace their judgment.

Why this matters

  • Free, individually accessible AI for clinicians sidesteps the need for an organizational contract, lowering the barrier for independent and smaller practices.
  • A new open benchmark with physician-authored test cases and deliberate red-teaming gives the medical AI field a rigorous, community-accessible standard for measuring real-world performance.
  • HIPAA compliance through a BAA, combined with a stated policy of not using clinician conversations for model training, addresses two of the most common concerns around AI adoption in healthcare settings.

Source note

  • The source is an official OpenAI product announcement page published April 22, 2026, with inline testimonials from named clinicians and citations to an AMA survey, Stanford’s MedHELM, MedMarks, and a HealthBench Professional PDF. Several key claims——including the AMA physician AI usage figures and GPT-5.4’s benchmark edge over human physicians——are drawn from OpenAI’s own reporting and its HealthBench Professional paper rather than independent verification, and the free tier is currently U.S.-only with expansion described only in general terms.
