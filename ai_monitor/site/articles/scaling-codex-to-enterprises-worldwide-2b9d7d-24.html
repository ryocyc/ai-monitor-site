Scaling Codex to enterprises worldwide

OpenAI is partnering with major global systems integrators and launching Codex Labs to help enterprises deploy its coding AI tool at scale.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has announced Codex Labs, a program that brings OpenAI experts directly into organizations through hands-on workshops and working sessions to help teams integrate Codex into real workflows. The company says the goal is to help enterprises move from early usage to repeatable deployment and get measurable value from the tool faster. Organizations can apply by filling out a form or reaching out to their OpenAI team.

To meet surging enterprise demand, OpenAI is also partnering with seven global systems integrators—Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS). These firms are tasked with helping enterprise customers identify high-value Codex use cases across the software development lifecycle and move from pilots to production-ready deployments. Accenture's Chief AI Officer, Lan Guan, said the firm is using Codex internally to move from static requirements to working solutions in hours rather than weeks, enabling faster prototyping and iteration for clients.

The announcement also updates Codex's reach: more than 4 million developers are now using the tool weekly, up from 3 million just two weeks prior. Early enterprise adopters include Virgin Atlantic, Ramp, Notion, Cisco, and Rakuten, which are using Codex across code review, test coverage, incident response, and large repository reasoning. OpenAI notes that Codex is increasingly extending beyond pure coding into browser-based work, image generation, and knowledge-work tasks across tools and applications.

Why this matters

  • Seven major GSIs are now officially trained to deploy Codex at enterprise scale, creating a clear channel for large organizations to adopt the tool without building in-house expertise first.
  • Weekly developer usage has crossed 4 million, showing rapid individual adoption that enterprises are now looking to systematize across their engineering orgs.
  • Codex is explicitly expanding beyond coding into broader knowledge work, signaling OpenAI's intent to position it as a general enterprise productivity layer rather than just a developer tool.
