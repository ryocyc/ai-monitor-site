OpenAI Launches Free ChatGPT for Clinicians, Citing AMA Data on Rising AI Use in Medicine

OpenAI is offering verified U.S. physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists free access to ChatGPT for Clinicians, a version of the AI assistant built for clinical documentation, medical research, and care consultations.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI announced on April 22, 2026, that it is making ChatGPT for Clinicians freely available to verified U.S. healthcare professionals — physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists. The product is designed to support tasks including documentation, medical research, and care consultations, with advanced AI models, reusable clinical skills, and cited search across peer-reviewed medical sources.

The launch comes as AI adoption among U.S. physicians has surged, according to an American Medical Association survey cited in the announcement. Physician use of AI in clinical practice reached 72% in 2026, up from 48% the prior year. OpenAI says clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past year, and it worked with hundreds of physician advisors to shape the product's feature set. Optional HIPAA compliance via a Business Associate Agreement is also available for eligible accounts, and conversations are not used to train models.

As part of the release, OpenAI introduced HealthBench Professional — an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks across care consult, documentation, and research use cases. In pre-release testing, physicians reviewed 6,924 conversations in their own workflows and rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. On a curated subset of 355 examples, GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperformed base GPT-5.4, other external models, and human physicians who had unbounded time and web access. A third of benchmark examples involved physicians deliberately red-teaming the models, and the dataset was selected to be 3.5x harder than typical conversations.

Why this matters

  • AI adoption among U.S. physicians has crossed 70%, and this free tier extends OpenAI's clinical footprint to individual practitioners outside enterprise contracts.
  • The HealthBench Professional benchmark provides a transparent, physician-reviewed yardstick for evaluating AI systems in real clinical chat scenarios, including adversarial testing.
  • The overlap of CME tracking, cited medical search, and optional HIPAA compliance in a single free product could shift how independent clinicians interact with AI tools.
