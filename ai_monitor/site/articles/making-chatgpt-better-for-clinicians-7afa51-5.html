OpenAI Makes ChatGPT for Clinicians Free for Verified U.S. Physicians, NPs, PAs, and Pharmacists

OpenAI has opened free access to ChatGPT for Clinicians, a purpose-built version of its AI assistant for documentation, medical research, and care consultation, targeting the growing number of healthcare workers using AI in daily practice.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI on April 22, 2026 announced that ChatGPT for Clinicians—a version of its AI assistant tailored for medical tasks—is now free for verified individual clinicians in the United States. Eligible users include physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists. The product covers clinical use cases such as care consultation, documentation, and medical literature review. A free tier grants access to OpenAI's frontier models for healthcare applications, reusable skills for repeatable workflows like referral letters and prior authorizations, cited answers drawn from peer-reviewed medical sources, and deep research across medical journals. An optional HIPAA-compliant option, backed by a Business Associate Agreement, is also available for eligible accounts.

The launch arrives as AI adoption among U.S. physicians has surged. An American Medical Association survey cited by OpenAI found that 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. Clinician usage of ChatGPT specifically has more than doubled over the past twelve months. OpenAI says physician advisors reviewed more than 700,000 model responses before and during the rollout, with a new response audited by a physician every few minutes. In pre-release testing, physicians evaluated 6,924 conversations spanning clinical care, documentation, and research, rating 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. On a subset of 355 examples validated by three independent physicians, ChatGPT for Clinicians cited sources more often than the physicians themselves did. The underlying model, GPT-5.4, has ranked top among systems evaluated on Stanford's MedHELM and MedMarks third-party benchmarks.

Alongside the free tier, OpenAI is releasing HealthBench Professional—an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks across care consultation, documentation, and medical research. Unlike general health evaluation sets, HealthBench Professional uses physician-authored conversations, multi-stage adjudication, and intentionally selects conversations that are 3.5 times more difficult than average to challenge models. OpenAI says GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperforms both base GPT-5.4 and human physicians given unbounded time and web access. The benchmark dataset is publicly downloadable. International rollout is planned in partnership with the Better Evidence Network, beginning with a pilot for verified clinicians outside the U.S. as local regulations permit.

Why this matters

  • With 72% of physicians now using AI in clinical practice—up from 48% a year ago—OpenAI's free offering signals that AI support for frontline clinicians is becoming mainstream rather than experimental.
  • The HealthBench Professional benchmark gives the research and clinical community a standardized, publicly available way to evaluate AI systems on real clinician tasks, raising the bar for transparency and reproducibility in health AI.
  • Optional HIPAA compliance via BAA extends the product's reach into settings where protected health information is involved, a critical gate for adoption in hospital systems and large group practices.
