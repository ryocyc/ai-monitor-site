MiniMax Launches Music 2.6 with Cover Feature and M2.7 Agent with Self-Improvement

MiniMax has rolled out Music 2.6, introducing a Cover feature for remixing melodies with enhanced bass and instrumentation, alongside the M2.7 Agent model that adds recursive self-improvement capabilities.

MiniMax Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: minimax-blog::identity::minimax-blog-minimax-music-26-bri Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

MiniMax Music 2.6 introduces a Cover feature that lets users take any melody and re-render it with deeper bass and richer instrumentation. The updated model supports a wide range of genres including Pop, Hyperpop, Dance/Club Electronic, Drive/Sports, Trap, Video Scoring, EDM, and Epic Game. Both the Cover feature and the underlying Music 2.6 model are accessible via MiniMax's API.

The M2.7 Agent model is positioned as a significant step up from M2.5, with MiniMax claiming substantial improvements in practical, real-world tasks. Its defining new capability is self-improvement — the ability to recursively refine its own outputs. The model is billed as having strong engineering and coding capabilities and supports complex office workflows such as multi-step Excel, Word, and PowerPoint operations with multi-round editing.

Both new releases are listed prominently on MiniMax's main product page, alongside the company's existing model lineup spanning text (M2 series), speech (Speech 2.8), video (Hailuo 2.3), and music generation. MiniMax is also advertising a new "Token Plan" pricing package aimed at developers, as well as a 2025 Financial Results announcement titled 'Towards an AI Era Platform Company.'

Why this matters

  • The self-improvement capability in M2.7 marks a new tier for MiniMax's Agent line, potentially reducing the need for human retry loops in coding and office automation tasks.
  • MiniMax Music 2.6's Cover feature gives developers a remixing tool within a music generation API, which is a relatively rare offering among AI music APIs.
  • With both products now live and accessible via API, developers can integrate enhanced music remixing and self-improving agent capabilities into production workflows immediately.
