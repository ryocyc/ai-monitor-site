OpenAI Opens GPT-5.5 to Verified Cybersecurity Defenders via Trusted Access Program

OpenAI has added GPT-5.5 and a domain-specific GPT-5.5-Cyber variant to its Trusted Access for Cyber program, expanding access to vetted defenders working on vulnerability research and critical infrastructure protection.

Article body

OpenAI has expanded its Trusted Access for Cyber initiative to include two new model offerings: GPT-5.5 and a specialized GPT-5.5-Cyber variant, both available to verified cybersecurity defenders through the company's existing trusted-access framework. The announcement was published to the OpenAI Newsroom on May 7, 2026.

The Trusted Access for Cyber program is OpenAI's gatekept pathway that allows approved security researchers and defenders—not the general public—to use frontier language models for tasks such as vulnerability discovery, analysis, and hardening of critical systems. The addition of GPT-5.5 and a cyber-domain-specific model reflects OpenAI's continued investment in tailoring frontier capabilities to high-stakes defensive use cases rather than offering broad public access.

For defenders working on real-world security challenges, access to GPT-5.5-class reasoning inside a verified, controlled environment could meaningfully accelerate vulnerability research workflows, particularly on complex targets where manual analysis is slow and resource-intensive.

Why this matters

  • The cyber-specific GPT-5.5-Cyber variant signals that OpenAI is investing in domain-specialized model configurations for high-stakes defensive tasks, not just general-purpose releases.
  • The trusted-access model keeps frontier AI capabilities out of unvetted hands while still enabling legitimate security research, a balancing act that shapes the competitive landscape for AI-augmented offensive and defensive security work.
