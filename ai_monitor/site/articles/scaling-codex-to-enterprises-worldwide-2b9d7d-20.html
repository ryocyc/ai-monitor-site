OpenAI Launches Codex Labs and GSI Partner Network to Scale Enterprise Adoption

OpenAI is pairing its new Codex Labs advisory offering with a roster of global systems integrators to help enterprises move Codex from pilot to production.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI is launching Codex Labs, a program that deploys OpenAI experts directly into enterprise organizations to help them integrate Codex into real workflows. The company also announced partnerships with seven global systems integrators (GSIs) — Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services — to extend that support at scale. According to the post, enterprise demand is already outpacing OpenAI's ability to serve it alone, and the GSI network is meant to close that gap. The partner firms are already using Codex internally to develop repeatable deployment practices before bringing it to their own clients.

The company is also sharing updated adoption numbers: more than 4 million developers now use Codex weekly, up from the 3 million reported just two weeks earlier. Enterprise customers named in the post include Virgin Atlantic, which is using Codex to increase test coverage and team velocity; Ramp, which is using it in code review; Notion, which is using it to build new features; Cisco, which is using it to reason across large interconnected code repositories; and Rakuten, which is using it for incident response. The post also notes that use is beginning to expand beyond engineering teams, into tasks such as drafting briefs, plans, and follow-ups across different tools.

Codex Labs itself is designed as a hands-on, workshop-driven engagement model, with OpenAI staff working inside organizations to identify high-value use cases and move them toward repeatable deployment. The seven GSI partners are described as experienced in modernizing software delivery and supporting organizational change at scale. Enterprises interested in Codex Labs can submit interest via a form on OpenAI's site or reach out to their OpenAI account team.

Why this matters

  • OpenAI's partnership with seven major GSIs signals a deliberate push to make Codex a standard enterprise tool, not just a developer-facing product.
  • Named enterprise customers across aviation, fintech, productivity, networking, and e-commerce show Codex is being evaluated for real production workloads, not just experimentation.
  • OpenAI's explicit mention of Codex expanding beyond coding into knowledge work tasks suggests the product is being repositioned as a broader enterprise productivity platform.
