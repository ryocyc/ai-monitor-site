OpenAI Launches Free ChatGPT for Clinicians, Citing Strong Safety Ratings and Outperformance Over Human Physicians

OpenAI is offering free ChatGPT access to verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States, alongside a new open benchmark it says its models outperform human doctors on.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has released ChatGPT for Clinicians, a purpose-built version of its AI assistant aimed at documentation, care consultation, and medical research. The product is now free to any individually verified U.S. physician, NP, PA, or pharmacist; a HIPAA-compliant option via Business Associate Agreement is available for eligible accounts. The rollout expands on OpenAI's existing ChatGPT for Healthcare offering, which serves health-system organizations at scale.

The release is backed by a newly introduced open benchmark called HealthBench Professional, which evaluates AI on physician-authored chat scenarios across care consult, writing and documentation, and medical research. In pre-release testing, physician advisors reviewed 6,924 conversations in their own clinical workflows and rated 99.6% of responses safe and accurate. On a curated subset of 355 examples where three independent physicians provided ground-truth citations, GPT-5.4 cited those sources more often than the physicians themselves. On the benchmark, GPT-5.4 outperformed base GPT-5.4, all other tested OpenAI and external models, and human physicians producing responses with unbounded time and web access.

Clinician adoption of AI is accelerating: an American Medical Association survey released this year found 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. OpenAI says usage of ChatGPT among clinicians worldwide has more than doubled in the past year. Access to ChatGPT for Clinicians is currently U.S.-only, with expansion planned through the Better Evidence Network for international clinicians pending local regulatory clearance. A Health Blueprint document accompanying the release offers OpenAI's recommendations for responsible AI integration in U.S. healthcare.

Why this matters

  • A large and growing share of U.S. physicians are already using AI tools, making the quality and safety of clinical-grade AI a pressing concern for patient care.
  • OpenAI's own benchmark testing claims GPT-5.4 outperforms human physicians in citation accuracy and overall benchmark scores, suggesting frontier AI may be approaching clinical-grade performance on real clinician tasks.
  • Free individual clinician access lowers the barrier to adoption outside large health-system contracts, potentially bringing AI assistance to a much broader range of clinical settings.
