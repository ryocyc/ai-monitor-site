OpenAI Makes ChatGPT for Clinicians Free for Verified U.S. Healthcare Professionals

OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Clinicians — a clinical-focused version of ChatGPT available at no cost to verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI is making ChatGPT for Clinicians available for free to verified individual clinicians in the United States. The product is designed for tasks like documentation, care consultation, and medical research, aiming to reduce administrative burden and free up time for patient care. Clinicians can access frontier AI models through a dedicated workspace with tools built specifically for healthcare workflows — including reusable skills for referral letters, prior authorization, and patient instructions.

The launch comes alongside new research showing that AI adoption among physicians has surged: 72% of physicians now report using AI in clinical practice, up from 48% a year prior, according to a 2026 survey by the American Medical Association. ChatGPT clinician usage has more than doubled over the same period. OpenAI says it worked with hundreds of physician advisors to shape the feature set, and that its physician reviewers evaluate a new model response every few minutes, having reviewed more than 700,000 to date.

OpenAI is also releasing HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks across care consult, writing and documentation, and medical research. In testing, OpenAI's GPT‑5.4 — the model powering ChatGPT for Clinicians — outperformed human physicians, other OpenAI and external models, and base GPT‑5.4 on the benchmark. Physicians rated 99.6% of responses from 6,924 tested conversations as safe and accurate. Optional HIPAA support via a Business Associate Agreement is available for eligible accounts.

Why this matters

  • Free access could significantly lower the barrier for individual clinicians — not just large health systems — to use AI in daily practice.
  • The 72% physician AI adoption figure signals a tipping point in clinical AI adoption, up sharply from 48% a year earlier.
  • Publishing HealthBench Professional as an open benchmark gives the broader research community a shared way to measure progress in clinical AI.

Source note

  • The announcement was published on OpenAI's newsroom on April 22, 2026. The free version is currently limited to verified U.S. clinicians; expansion to other countries is planned via a pilot with the Better Evidence Network. HealthBench Professional's dataset is publicly available for download.
