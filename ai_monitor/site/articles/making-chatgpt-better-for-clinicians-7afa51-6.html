Making ChatGPT better for clinicians

OpenAI is making a clinical-purpose version of ChatGPT available for free to verified U.S. physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

ChatGPT for Clinicians, announced April 22, 2026, is built specifically for tasks like documentation, medical research, and care consultation. The free tier gives individual verified clinicians in the United States access to frontier AI models, reusable skills for repeatable workflows such as prior authorizations and referral letters, and real-time search across peer-reviewed medical sources. Clinicians can also delegate journal reviews to ChatGPT and receive cited, comprehensive reports in return, and eligible evidence review can count toward continuing medical education credits.

The announcement arrives as a 2026 American Medical Association survey reports 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. Clinician usage of ChatGPT more than doubled over the past year, and OpenAI says it worked with hundreds of physician advisors to shape the tool's feature set. Before launch, physician advisors tested the product across 6,924 real-world conversations spanning clinical care, documentation, and research; 99.6% of responses were rated safe and accurate by physicians.

OpenAI simultaneously released HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for clinician-facing AI tasks, alongside a paper and downloadable dataset. On the benchmark, GPT‑5.4 outperformed base GPT‑5.4, all other OpenAI and external models tested, and human physicians working in their own specialties with unlimited time and web access. The free offering currently covers U.S. clinicians only, with plans to expand internationally via a pilot with the Better Evidence Network.

Why this matters

  • AI adoption among U.S. physicians has surged to 72%, making clinical-grade AI tools increasingly central to everyday practice.
  • The benchmark results claim GPT‑5.4 outperforms human physicians in real clinician chat tasks, representing a notable benchmark milestone for AI in healthcare.
  • Free access for verified individual clinicians removes a cost barrier that previously limited such tools largely to large health systems with enterprise agreements.
