Scaling Codex to enterprises worldwide

OpenAI is pairing Codex Labs — a direct-engagement program staffed by its own experts — with a roster of seven major global systems integrators to accelerate enterprise rollouts of its coding and knowledge-work agent.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI announced it is launching Codex Labs, a program that puts OpenAI experts directly inside organizations to run workshops and working sessions aimed at moving Codex from early pilots into repeatable, production-level deployment. The company frames the goal simply: help enterprises extract real value from Codex faster than they could on their own.

To broaden its reach beyond what its own team can handle, OpenAI simultaneously announced partnerships with seven global systems integrators — Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services. These firms are already using Codex internally and are being trained to identify high-value use cases for enterprise clients across the software development lifecycle, from prototyping through production. Lan Guan, Accenture's Chief AI Officer, said the firm's professionals are using Codex to go from static requirements to working solutions in hours rather than weeks.

The push comes amid rapid developer uptake: OpenAI says more than 4 million developers were using Codex weekly as of mid-April, up from 3 million just two weeks earlier. Early enterprise customers include Virgin Atlantic, which is using Codex to increase test coverage and team velocity; Ramp, which is accelerating code review; Notion, which is building new features faster; Cisco, which is reasoning across large interconnected repositories; and Rakuten, which is applying it to incident response. OpenAI notes that Codex is also expanding beyond pure coding into browser-based work, image generation, and cross-tool knowledge tasks — a reach that extends its relevance beyond engineering teams.

Why this matters

  • A roster of seven named GSIs gives enterprises ready-made implementation partners, removing a common bottleneck in enterprise AI rollouts.
  • The jump from 3 million to 4 million weekly Codex developers in roughly two weeks signals a usage curve that OpenAI clearly wants to reinforce with structured enterprise support.
  • Expanding Codex beyond coding into knowledge-work tasks like drafting, reasoning, and cross-tool workflows broadens the addressable market inside any organization — not just its engineering division.
