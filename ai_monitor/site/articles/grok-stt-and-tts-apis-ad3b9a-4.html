xAI Launches Grok Speech to Text and Text to Speech APIs

xAI has released two standalone audio APIs, Grok Speech to Text and Grok Text to Speech, giving developers direct access to the speech technology already embedded in Grok Voice, Tesla vehicles, and Starlink customer support.

xAI Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: xai-blog::identity::grok-stt-and-tts-apis Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

xAI announced on April 17, 2026 the general availability of two new standalone audio APIs: Grok Speech to Text (STT) and Grok Text to Speech (TTS). Both endpoints are accessible via REST and WebSocket, allowing developers to embed transcription and voice synthesis into applications such as voice agents, real-time captioning tools, accessibility products, podcasts, and interactive audio experiences. The APIs share the infrastructure that already powers Grok Voice inside Tesla vehicles and Starlink customer support.

The Speech to Text API is priced at $0.10 per audio hour for batch transcription and $0.20 per audio hour for real-time streaming, with no hidden fees. The Text to Speech API costs $4.20 per one million characters. The STT API covers more than 25 languages, supports multichannel audio with speaker diarization, and includes word-level timestamps. A comparative table in the announcement shows Grok STT achieving an overall word error rate of 6.9%, compared to 9.0% for ElevenLabs, 11.0% for Deepgram, and 12.9% for AssemblyAI on equivalent benchmarks. Inverse Text Normalization is also available as an optional formatting layer, converting raw spoken numbers, dates, and currencies into structured output.

On the TTS side, xAI emphasizes expressive voice control through simple inline speech tags such as `[laugh]`, `[sigh]`, `[whisper]`, and semantic tags for pacing and emphasis. No complex markup is required. Both APIs are accessible through the xAI developer console, which includes a dedicated voice playground for the TTS endpoint. Links to documentation and the API console are provided directly in the announcement.

Why this matters

  • xAI is entering the crowded speech API market with aggressive pricing and benchmark numbers that claim to beat leading providers on transcription accuracy.
  • The APIs are built on infrastructure already running in Tesla vehicles and Starlink customer support, suggesting real-world stress-testing at scale.
  • Both endpoints are offered as standalone products rather than bundled features, making it straightforward for any developer to evaluate and integrate them without committing to a larger platform.
