OpenAI Scales Codex to Enterprises Worldwide with New Partner Program

OpenAI is partnering with seven global systems integrators to help businesses deploy Codex at scale, as weekly developer usage surpasses 4 million.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched Codex Transformation Partners, a coordinated enterprise rollout effort that pairs its coding tool with systems integrators who can guide large organizations from pilot to production. The initiative aims to close a growing gap between demand for Codex and OpenAI's capacity to onboard enterprise customers directly. Seven major global systems integrators (GSIs) are now part of the program: Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS). These firms will help customers identify high-value Codex use cases across the software development lifecycle, including code review, test coverage, and incident response.

Enterprise adoption is already visible across a range of industries. OpenAI cited Virgin Atlantic using Codex to increase test coverage and team velocity, Ramp for accelerating code review, Notion for rapidly building new features, Cisco for reasoning across large interconnected repositories, and Rakuten for incident response. The company noted that momentum is spreading beyond engineering teams as workers use Codex to synthesize information across tools, generate briefs, plans, and drafts, and take action on that output.

OpenAI is also deploying its own experts through Codex Labs, which runs hands-on workshops and working sessions inside organizations. The company acknowledged that enterprise demand is outpacing its ability to onboard customers at the speed they want, making the GSI network a critical part of the scaling strategy. Accenture's Chief AI Officer Lan Guan highlighted the practical effect: professionals are moving from static requirements to working solutions in hours rather than weeks.

Why this matters

  • Weekly Codex users have grown from 3 million to over 4 million in just two weeks, signaling rapid mainstream adoption beyond individual developers.
  • Seven major global systems integrators are now formally equipped to deploy Codex across large enterprise environments, which could significantly widen the tool's reach.
  • OpenAI is explicitly extending Codex beyond coding into knowledge-work tasks like browser-based work, image generation, and cross-tool reasoning, broadening the enterprise use case.
