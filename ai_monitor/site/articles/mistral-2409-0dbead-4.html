Mistral March Releases: Voxtral TTS, Mistral Small 4, Leanstral, Moderation 2603

Mistral shipped four notable releases in March, spanning speech synthesis, a unified small model, a formal proof agent, and a moderation update, alongside expanded guardrails controls.

Mistral Changelog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: mistral-changelog::identity::mistral-24.09 Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

Mistral released Voxtral TTS (voxtral-tts-2603) on March 23 — a text-to-speech model featuring zero-shot voice cloning, multilingual output, and real-time streaming. The company calls it state-of-the-art, though no independent benchmarks are referenced on the changelog page.

On March 16, Mistral Small 4 (mistral-small-2603) arrived as a hybrid model combining instruct, reasoning, and coding capabilities in a single multimodal package with a 256k context window. The same day, the company released Leanstral (labs-leanstral-2603), its first open-source code agent built for Lean 4 formal proof engineering — a niche but technically specific addition to the model lineup.

The March 12 release of Mistral Moderation 2603 (mistral-moderation-2603) rounds out the period. Separately, the changelog documents API-level guardrails expansion: Custom Guardrails can now be configured directly on an Agent, passed per-request on POST /v1/conversations, and passed per-request on POST /v1/chat/completions.

Why this matters

  • Voxtral TTS adds real-time streaming and voice cloning to Mistral's audio portfolio, following earlier Voxtral Small and Mini Transcribe releases.
  • Mistral Small 4's 256k context window and hybrid instruct/reasoning/coding design targets developers who need a compact all-rounder without switching models.
  • Leanstral's focus on Lean 4 formal proof engineering is an unusually specific domain — it signals Mistral is branching into mathematically rigorous code verification, not just general coding tasks.
