The post, authored by wing lian and published on April 30, 2026, focuses on the workflow for training models that use ternary weight quantization—where each parameter is represented by only three possible values, enabling far smaller model files than standard 16-bit or 8-bit formats. The guide references TII Axolotl, suggesting a connection to the Technology Innovation Institute's model variants. Ternary quantization sits at the aggressive end of the model compression spectrum, cutting weight precision to just two bits while attempting to preserve model quality through training-aware techniques. The Axolotl fine-tuning toolkit is widely used in the open-source LLM community to adapt base models to specific tasks and domains, supporting a range of quantization and training strategies.
