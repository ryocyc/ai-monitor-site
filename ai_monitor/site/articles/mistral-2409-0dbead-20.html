Mistral March Releases: Voxtral TTS, Mistral Small 4, Leanstral, Moderation 2603

Mistral shipped four notable releases in March, including a new text-to-speech model, a hybrid instruct/reasoning model, an open-source formal proof agent, and an updated moderation model.

Mistral Changelog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: mistral-changelog::identity::mistral-24.09 Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

On March 23, Mistral released Voxtral TTS (voxtral-tts-2603), a text-to-speech model described as state-of-the-art, featuring zero-shot voice cloning, multilingual support, and real-time streaming capabilities. This follows earlier Voxtral audio model releases and expands the company's footprint in voice AI.

March 16 brought two releases. The first, Mistral Small 4 (mistral-small-2603), is a hybrid model that unifies instruct, reasoning, and coding tasks in a single multimodal model with a 256,000-token context window. The second, Leanstral (labs-leanstral-2603), is Mistral's first open-source code agent purpose-built for Lean 4 formal proof engineering—a niche but growing area at the intersection of verification and AI.

On March 12, Mistral released Mistral Moderation 2603 (mistral-moderation-2603) and added Custom Guardrails support for both Agents and Conversations, allowing developers to configure guardrails directly on an agent or pass them per-request on the /v1/conversations and /v1/chat/completions endpoints.

Why this matters

  • Voxtral TTS marks Mistral's entry into production-grade text-to-speech with voice cloning, directly competing with providers like ElevenLabs and OpenAI.
  • Mistral Small 4's 256k context window and hybrid reasoning/coding design position it for long-document and agentic use cases.
  • Leanstral being open-source could lower the barrier for formal verification work in the Lean 4 ecosystem, which has growing adoption in mathematics and software correctness research.
