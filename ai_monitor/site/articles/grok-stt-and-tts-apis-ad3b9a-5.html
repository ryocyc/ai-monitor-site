Grok Speech to Text and Text to Speech APIs Now Available

xAI has released two standalone audio APIs — Grok Speech to Text and Grok Text to Speech — built on the infrastructure powering Grok Voice, Tesla vehicles, and Starlink customer support.

xAI Blog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: xai-blog::identity::grok-stt-and-tts-apis Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

The Grok Speech to Text API handles both batch transcription via REST API and real-time streaming via WebSocket, targeting developers building voice agents, transcription tools, accessibility features, podcasts, and interactive audio experiences. It offers word-level timestamps, speaker diarization, and multichannel support, along with Inverse Text Normalization that converts spoken numbers, dates, and currencies into structured output. The company benchmarks Grok STT against ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and AssemblyAI on phone calls, meetings, video/podcasts, and telephone domains, posting an overall word error rate of 6.9% compared to 9.0%, 11.0%, and 12.9% respectively.

Grok Text to Speech supports both REST and WebSocket endpoints and distinguishes itself with inline speech tags — `[laugh]`, `[sigh]`, `[whisper]`, ` `, and others — that let developers add prosody and emotion to generated audio without complex markup. The TTS system is described as fast, natural, and expressive, and the API covers 25 or more languages.

Pricing is deliberately straightforward: STT costs $0.10 per audio hour for batch jobs and $0.20 per hour for streaming, while TTS is priced at $4.20 per 1 million characters, with no stated hidden fees. Both products are available now through the xAI API console and developer documentation.

Why this matters

  • xAI is positioning Grok STT directly against established transcription providers, backing the claims with public benchmark figures across multiple audio domains.
  • The TTS speech-tag system offers a developer-friendly way to add emotional nuance to generated voice without building custom prosody models from scratch.
  • Pricing for STT is competitive at the batch tier, while the TTS rate of $4.20 per million characters sits within the range of other commercial voice synthesis services.
