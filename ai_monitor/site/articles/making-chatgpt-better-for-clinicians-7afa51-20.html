OpenAI Makes ChatGPT Free for Verified U.S. Clinicians

OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a purpose-built version of its AI assistant available at no cost to verified U.S. physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

The new offering, announced April 22, 2026, gives qualifying clinicians free access to OpenAI's current frontier models with features tailored to clinical workflows, including cited medical search, reusable skills for tasks like prior authorization letters and referral documentation, and the ability to delegate literature reviews across peer-reviewed sources. Optional HIPAA compliance is available for accounts that handle protected health information via a Business Associate Agreement.

The launch is accompanied by a new open benchmark called HealthBench Professional, designed specifically for real clinician chat tasks across three domains: care consultation, writing and documentation, and medical research. According to OpenAI, physician advisors reviewed more than 700,000 model responses during development, and pre-release testing involved 6,924 conversations across clinical care, documentation, and research. Physicians rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. On a curated subset of 355 examples where three independent physicians provided ground-truth citations, ChatGPT for Clinicians cited those sources more often than the physicians themselves. Across the full benchmark, GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperformed base GPT-5.4, all other OpenAI and external models, and human physicians given unbounded time and web access.

The release lands amid rapidly rising AI adoption among U.S. physicians. A 2026 American Medical Association survey found 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year, with millions of clinicians already using ChatGPT weekly for care consultation, documentation, and research. Clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past year. OpenAI says it plans to expand access to additional countries over time, beginning with a pilot through the Better Evidence Network as permitted by local regulations.

Why this matters

  • A 2026 AMA survey puts physician AI adoption at 72%, up from 48% the prior year, signaling AI has crossed into mainstream clinical practice—and this free offering could accelerate that trajectory further.
  • The HealthBench Professional benchmark shows GPT-5.4 outperforming both external models and human physicians on cited accuracy, providing the first public third-party comparison of its kind in a clinical context.
  • Free access to frontier AI models for clinicians removes a cost barrier that has historically limited adoption in smaller or lower-resourced practices, potentially widening the gap between large health systems and independent providers.
