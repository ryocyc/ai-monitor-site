Runway Launches Seedance 2.0, Expanding Its Multimodal AI Video Platform

Runway has released Seedance 2.0, a video generation and editing model accepting text, image, video, or audio as inputs, now available to Unlimited and Enterprise customers outside the US.

Runway Changelog <span data-utc="2026-04-28T12:47:09+00:00">2026-04-28 12:47 UTC</span> Key: evt-d1f6f05a885dbbb32ef1 Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

Runway on April 7, 2026 made Seedance 2.0 broadly available to its Unlimited and Enterprise plan holders outside the United States. The model allows users to generate or edit video from any combination of text prompts, images, existing video, or audio inputs—a multimodal workflow that consolidates what previously required separate tools or pipeline steps into a single interface.

The release sits alongside several other significant additions to the Runway platform over the past several months. In March, Runway introduced Characters, real-time intelligent avatars accessible via the company's API and web demo. February brought Nano Banana 2, described as Runway's most consistent image generation and editing model to date, and a third-party model hub that now includes Kling 3.0, Kling 2.6 Pro, WAN2.2 Animate, GPT-Image-1.5, and Sora 2 Pro among others. Together these additions signal a platform strategy focused on breadth—both in input modalities and in the range of underlying models available to users.

For creative teams and application builders, Seedance 2.0's multimodal input model is likely the most immediately practical development, as it reduces friction between concept and output. The Runway API access granted alongside Characters and other recent releases also opens the door for developers to embed Runway's capabilities into external products, while the third-party model aggregation positions the platform as a consolidation point rather than a single-model tool.

Why this matters

  • Seedance 2.0's unified multimodal input (text, image, video, audio) in a single model reduces the need for separate pipeline stages in video production workflows.
  • Runway's third-party model hub gives users access to rival generators—Kling, Sora, GPT-Image—without leaving the Runway interface, raising competitive pressure on platforms that offer only proprietary models.
  • The expansion of API-accessible features (Characters, and by extension the broader platform) opens direct integration opportunities for developers building AI-powered creative or avatar-based applications.
