Runway Rolls Out Annual Billing With 20% Discount Across AI Video Tiers

Runway has introduced a yearly billing option across its plan lineup, offering a 20% discount on its Free, Standard, Pro, and Unlimited tiers — a move that locks in lower per-month rates for creators and teams who commit annually to its generative video and image platform.

Article body

Runway's pricing page now surfaces a yearly billing toggle alongside monthly options, with the discount applying across all four consumer tiers. The Free plan remains at $0 per month with a one-time allocation of 125 credits, while the Standard plan is listed at $15 per month for individual users, rising to $35 for the Pro tier and $95 for Unlimited. Switching to annual billing reduces those per-user rates accordingly.

The Standard plan includes 625 credits per month — sufficient for roughly 52 seconds of Gen-4 video or 125 seconds of the faster Gen-4 Turbo model — alongside access to Gen-4 image generation, Generative Audio, Text to Speech, and 5 GB of asset storage. It supports workspaces of up to five users. The Pro tier adds broader feature access intended for individuals and teams integrating Runway more heavily into their content workflows, while the Unlimited plan removes credit caps entirely for heavy users.

For studios, agencies, or independent creators who run significant video output through Runway, the annual commitment meaningfully lowers the effective cost per credit. The discount may also serve as a retention lever — locking in annual subscribers reduces churn risk while giving Runway predictable revenue with which to support ongoing model development.

Why this matters

  • Runway is a widely used platform for AI-powered video and image generation among content creators, marketers, and post-production teams; pricing changes directly affect their operating costs.
  • The 20% annual discount narrows the gap between Runway's per-credit cost and competitors' subscription models, making long-term commitments more attractive for high-volume users.
  • The credit-per-second model across different model tiers gives users concrete language for estimating monthly spend — a transparency improvement over flat-rate unlimited tiers from some competitors.
