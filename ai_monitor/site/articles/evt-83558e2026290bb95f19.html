LightOn AI Open-Sources DenseOn and LateOn, Claiming State-of-the-Art Results for Single and Multi-Vector Retrieval Models

LightOn AI has published DenseOn and LateOn, a pair of open embedding techniques that the company says push past prior benchmarks for both single-vector and multi-vector retrieval setups, with models and code shared via Hugging Face.

Article body

LightOn AI posted a new article on the Hugging Face blog this week introducing DenseOn and LateOn, a pair of techniques and model variants aimed at dense and late-interaction retrieval. The post appeared on the community blog on April 21, 2026, authored by Raphael Sourty, Antoine Chaffin, Paulo Moura, and Amélie Chatelain. The work is billed as open state-of-the-art for both single-vector and multi-vector configurations.

DenseOn appears to address single-vector retrieval, where each document or passage is mapped to a single embedding vector — the dominant paradigm in standard semantic search. LateOn targets multi-vector setups, in which each token in a passage has its own representation, allowing richer cross-encoding of query-document token interactions at the cost of higher compute. The post links to model weights on Hugging Face, making the checkpoints directly accessible to the community.

For teams building retrieval-augmented pipelines, semantic search backends, or reranking layers, the release offers an open alternative to proprietary models in both the fast single-vector and the higher-fidelity multi-vector regimes. Inference engineers and RAG developers working on Hugging Face would be the primary audience; the models slot into existing dense retrieval frameworks as drop-in checkpoints.

Why this matters

  • Both model families are released as open weights on Hugging Face, giving teams a free, self-hostable option for dense retrieval without API lock-in.
  • Covering both single-vector and multi-vector paradigms in one release means practitioners can compare the accuracy-latency tradeoff within the same model family rather than across vendors.
  • LateOn targets the multi-vector / late-interaction setting used in reranking and high-accuracy retrieval pipelines, where query-document token-level cross-encoding matters.
