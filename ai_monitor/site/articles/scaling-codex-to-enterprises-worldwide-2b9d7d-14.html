OpenAI Scales Codex to Enterprises Worldwide with New Transformation Partner Network

OpenAI is expanding Codex into enterprise workflows through a new program pairing organizations with global systems integrators to accelerate adoption at scale.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI's Codex coding assistant is now being used by more than 4 million developers per week, up from 3 million just two weeks prior, signaling a sharp acceleration in both individual and enterprise adoption. Companies including Virgin Atlantic, Ramp, Notion, Cisco, and Rakuten are already embedding Codex across their engineering operations — spanning test coverage, code review, feature development, repository reasoning, and incident response.

Beyond traditional coding tasks, Codex is gaining traction in broader knowledge-work roles. OpenAI says teams are using it to pull context from disparate tools, reason through priorities, and produce actionable outputs like briefs, plans, and checklists — then execute those actions directly. A separate use-cases page documents Codex's expanding support for browser-based work, image generation, and cross-tool memory.

To capitalize on enterprise demand, OpenAI is launching Codex Labs — a team of OpenAI experts embedded in organizations through hands-on workshops and working sessions — alongside a network of global systems integrators. The initial partner roster includes Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services. These firms will help customers move from pilot to production-ready Codex deployments, and several are already using Codex internally to refine their own delivery practices. Accenture's chief AI officer noted that professionals are using Codex to produce working solutions from static requirements in hours rather than weeks.

Why this matters

  • The weekly developer count jumped from 3 million to 4 million in under two weeks, suggesting viral momentum that may reshape how software is built across industries.
  • Enterprise adoption is extending beyond engineering — Codex is now being used for knowledge-work tasks like drafting, planning, and cross-tool reasoning, which widens the addressable market significantly.
  • OpenAI's partner network of seven global systems integrators is designed to solve the execution bottleneck that has historically slowed enterprise AI rollouts, potentially accelerating Codex's path to production at scale.
