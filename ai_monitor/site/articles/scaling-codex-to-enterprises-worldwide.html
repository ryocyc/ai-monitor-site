OpenAI Scales Codex to Enterprises Worldwide with Transformation Partners

OpenAI is expanding its enterprise push for Codex, a coding AI tool now used by more than 4 million developers weekly, by pairing organizations with a roster of global systems integrators to move from pilot to production.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI is rolling out a structured enterprise program called Codex Transformation Partners to help large organizations deploy Codex at scale. The effort combines dedicated OpenAI workshops through a new Codex Labs initiative with assistance from a cohort of seven global systems integrators: Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS). The goal is to move enterprises from early usage to repeatable, production-ready deployments faster than OpenAI can support on its own.

The push comes as adoption momentum is growing rapidly. In early April, OpenAI reported more than 3 million developers using Codex weekly; just two weeks later that figure has surpassed 4 million. Enterprises are now moving beyond individual developer use into broader rollout across software teams. Cited examples include Virgin Atlantic using Codex to increase test coverage and team velocity, Ramp accelerating code review, Notion building new features, Cisco reasoning across large interconnected repositories, and Rakuten handling incident response tasks.

Beyond engineering, OpenAI says Codex is being applied to knowledge-work tasks such as browser-based work, image generation, memory, and ongoing work across tools and apps. Teams are using it to pull together context from different sources, reason through what matters, and produce outputs like briefs, plans, drafts, and checklists. The company is betting that these expanding use cases will drive enterprise interest across the organization, not just within developer teams.

Why this matters

  • OpenAI's developer base for Codex has grown by roughly a third in under a month, signaling accelerating demand that is now spilling into enterprise deals.
  • The roster of seven major systems integrators gives OpenAI a channel to reach large enterprise customers that it could not serve efficiently through direct sales alone.
  • Expanding Codex use beyond coding into knowledge work signals OpenAI's intent to position the product as a broader enterprise productivity tool, not just a coding assistant.
