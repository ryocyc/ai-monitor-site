OpenAI Launches Codex Labs and Global Integrator Partners to Bring Codex to Enterprises Everywhere

OpenAI is pairing direct hands-on engagement through a new Codex Labs program with a roster of major global systems integrators to accelerate enterprise-wide deployment of its coding assistant.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI is launching Codex Labs, a service that sends OpenAI experts directly into organizations for hands-on workshops and working sessions aimed at getting teams from early Codex usage to repeatable, production-scale deployment. The company says the goal is simple: help enterprises extract real value from Codex faster.

To meet what it describes as demand outpacing its own capacity to help enterprises adopt the tool quickly, OpenAI is simultaneously partnering with a list of leading global systems integrators (GSIs): Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS). These firms are already using Codex internally and will help enterprise customers move from pilots to production-ready deployments across the software development lifecycle. Accenture's Chief AI Officer, Lan Guan, said in a quoted statement that the tool is enabling her firm's professionals to move from static requirements to working solutions 'in hours, not weeks.'

The push comes as OpenAI reports that developer usage of Codex has grown from 3 million weekly users in early April to more than 4 million two weeks later. Enterprise customers currently using Codex include Virgin Atlantic (increasing test coverage and team velocity), Ramp (accelerating code review), Notion (building new features), Cisco (reasoning across large interconnected codebases), and Rakuten (incident response). OpenAI also notes that Codex is moving beyond pure coding into browser-based work, image generation, memory, and cross-tool task management.

Why this matters

  • A dedicated enterprise engagement arm (Codex Labs) signals OpenAI is investing in the long-term commercial rollout of Codex beyond individual developers.
  • Seven major GSIs — including TCS, Accenture, and PwC — are now official Codex deployment partners, giving OpenAI a global enterprise delivery channel it didn't have before.
  • The broadening of Codex's scope from pure coding to cross-tool workflows (briefs, plans, checklists, drafts) suggests OpenAI is positioning it as a broader productivity layer for non-engineering teams too.
