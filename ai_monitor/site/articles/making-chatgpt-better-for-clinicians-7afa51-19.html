OpenAI Offers Free ChatGPT to Verified Healthcare Professionals

OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a free version of its AI assistant designed for clinical documentation, medical research, and care consultation, available to verified physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists in the United States.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI announced on April 22, 2026, that ChatGPT for Clinicians is now free for verified individual healthcare professionals in the United States. The product targets physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists, and is built specifically for clinical workflows such as documentation, medical research, and care consultation. A free version with frontier model access, trusted medical search, and deep research across peer-reviewed sources is the core of the offering, with optional HIPAA compliance support via a Business Associate Agreement for eligible accounts.

The launch comes alongside new data on physician AI adoption. According to a 2026 survey by the American Medical Association cited by OpenAI, 72% of physicians now report using AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. The company also says clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past year, and that it worked with hundreds of physician advisors to shape the product's capabilities—including reusable skills for tasks like referral letters, prior authorizations, and patient instructions.

OpenAI also introduced HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks across care consult, writing and documentation, and medical research. The company says its models, including GPT‑5.4, outperform human physicians on the benchmark, citing physician review of more than 700,000 model responses and a pre-release test of 6,924 conversations in daily clinical work where physicians rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate.

Why this matters

  • Millions of clinicians already use ChatGPT weekly; making a dedicated, free clinical version available to verified U.S. healthcare workers could significantly expand AI adoption in a high-stakes environment.
  • The 72% physician AI adoption rate reported by the AMA—up from 48% a year prior—signals a rapid shift in how clinical teams are integrating AI into day-to-day practice.
  • HealthBench Professional, released alongside the product, is an open benchmark with physician-authored data and multi-stage adjudication, giving the broader medical and AI community a shared yardstick for measuring clinical AI performance.
