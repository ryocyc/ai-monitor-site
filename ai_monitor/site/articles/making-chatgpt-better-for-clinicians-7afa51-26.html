ChatGPT for Clinicians Launches Free for Verified Physicians, NPs, PAs, and Pharmacists

OpenAI is offering its clinical-grade ChatGPT workspace at no cost to verified individual healthcare professionals in the United States, with plans to expand internationally.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a purpose-built version of ChatGPT tailored to documentation, medical research, and care-consult tasks. The product is free to any verified U.S. physician, nurse practitioner, physician assistant, or pharmacist, and is accessible via a dedicated signup path at chatgpt.com. The announcement, dated April 22, 2026, also introduced HealthBench Professional, a new open benchmark for real clinician conversations designed to measure model performance on care consult, writing and documentation, and medical research tasks.

The launch arrives against a backdrop of surging AI adoption among U.S. physicians. According to a 2026 American Medical Association survey cited in the post, 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. OpenAI says millions of clinicians worldwide use ChatGPT weekly, and that clinician usage has more than doubled over the past year. Before release, physician advisors tested 6,924 real conversations drawn from clinical care, documentation, and research workflows, rating 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. The models underpinning ChatGPT for Clinicians, including GPT-5.4, reportedly outperform both external models and human physicians on the HealthBench Professional benchmark.

ChatGPT for Clinicians includes features aimed at recurring clinical workflows: reusable skills for tasks like referral letters and prior authorization, cited answers from peer-reviewed medical sources, automated medical literature reviews, and optional HIPAA compliance via a Business Associate Agreement. Eligible clinicians can also have evidence review automatically count toward continuing medical education credits. Global expansion is planned through a pilot with the Better Evidence Network, working within local regulatory frameworks.

Why this matters

  • Physician AI adoption has crossed a majority threshold — 72% now use AI in clinical practice, up from under half a year ago.
  • Free, verified individual access removes a cost barrier that previously limited independent clinicians from deploying purpose-built AI tools at the point of care.
  • The simultaneous release of HealthBench Professional provides a transparent, open benchmark for measuring how AI systems perform on real clinician tasks, an area where rigorous third-party evaluation has been scarce.
