OpenAI Makes ChatGPT Free for Verified U.S. Clinicians

OpenAI is offering free ChatGPT access to verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States, targeting the mounting administrative burden weighing on healthcare workers.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI announced ChatGPT for Clinicians, a no-cost version of ChatGPT built for tasks like medical research, documentation, and care consultation. The product is available immediately to verified U.S. clinicians — physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists — with an optional HIPAA compliance pathway via a Business Associate Agreement for eligible accounts. The company says it worked with hundreds of physician advisors to shape the feature set, which includes trusted clinical search drawing on peer-reviewed medical sources, reusable skills for tasks such as prior authorization letters and referral notes, and deep research across medical journals. Eligible evidence review while using ChatGPT can also count toward continuing medical education credits.

The announcement arrives amid a sharp rise in AI adoption among U.S. physicians. An American Medical Association survey cited by OpenAI found that 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice — up from 48% the prior year — with clinician usage of ChatGPT more than doubling over the same period. OpenAI says its physician advisor team reviews more than 700,000 model responses covering real-world clinical use cases, adding that models like GPT-5.4 top third-party healthcare evaluations including Stanford's MedHELM and MedMarks.

Alongside the product, OpenAI released HealthBench Professional — an open benchmark for clinician chat tasks across care consultation, documentation, and medical research. In testing, 6,924 conversations were reviewed in real clinical workflows; physicians rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. OpenAI claims GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperforms both external models and human physicians as a baseline. The benchmark dataset and methodology are publicly available, though OpenAI asks that specific examples not be reproduced online to avoid contaminating future training corpora.

Why this matters

  • Free clinician access could significantly expand AI adoption in healthcare, given that AI use among physicians already jumped from 48% to 72% in one year.
  • HealthBench Professional offers an open, physician-reviewed benchmark for measuring AI performance in real clinical chat tasks — a gap the field has long lacked.
  • The optional HIPAA BAA widens the pathway for clinicians handling protected health information, addressing a key barrier to clinical AI adoption.
