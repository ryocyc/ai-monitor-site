Scaling Codex to enterprises worldwide

OpenAI is launching Codex Labs and teaming up with major global systems integrators to accelerate enterprise adoption of its AI coding tool.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has announced Codex Labs, a new program that brings OpenAI experts directly into organizations to help teams integrate Codex into real engineering workflows through hands-on workshops and working sessions. The company says the goal is to help enterprises move from early usage to repeatable, production-level deployment faster. Organizations interested in hosting a Codex Lab can fill out an interest form on OpenAI's website or contact their OpenAI team.

To meet growing enterprise demand, OpenAI is also partnering with leading global systems integrators including Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services. These firms will help enterprise customers identify high-value Codex use cases across the software development lifecycle and move from pilots to production-ready deployments. Accenture's Chief AI Officer, Lan Guan, cited examples of professionals using Codex to go "from static requirements to working solutions in hours, not weeks," enabling rapid prototyping and faster iteration.

The announcement comes as OpenAI reports that developer usage of Codex has grown from more than 3 million weekly users in early April to over 4 million just two weeks later. Enterprise customers already deploying Codex include Virgin Atlantic, which is using it to increase test coverage and team velocity; Ramp for code review; Notion for building new features; Cisco for reasoning across large interconnected repositories; and Rakuten for incident response. OpenAI notes that usage is also expanding beyond traditional coding into knowledge-work tasks like briefs, plans, and follow-ups across tools and applications.

Why this matters

  • OpenAI is formalizing enterprise deployment support through dedicated on-site engagement via Codex Labs, going beyond self-serve adoption.
  • Seven major global systems integrators — including Accenture, PwC, and TCS — are now positioned to deliver Codex at scale across large organizations worldwide.
  • Weekly developer usage of Codex crossed 4 million in mid-April, signaling strong bottom-up adoption that enterprises are now looking to institutionalize with structured rollout programs.
