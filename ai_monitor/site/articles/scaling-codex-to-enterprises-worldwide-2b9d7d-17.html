Scaling Codex to enterprises worldwide

OpenAI is launching Codex Labs and partnering with seven global systems integrators to help enterprises deploy Codex at scale, with over 4 million developers now using the tool weekly.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has introduced Codex Labs, a new initiative that brings OpenAI experts directly into organizations through hands-on workshops and working sessions, designed to move enterprises from early Codex usage to repeatable, production-level deployment. The company is also partnering with leading global systems integrators—including Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services—to scale that support and help customers move from pilots to production-ready implementations. These partners are themselves using Codex internally to build repeatable delivery methods for bringing the tool to enterprise clients.

The company reports that more than 4 million developers now use Codex every week, up from 3 million just two weeks earlier. Enterprises are deploying it across a range of use cases: Virgin Atlantic is using it to improve test coverage and team velocity; Ramp is using it to speed up code review; Notion is building new features with it; Cisco is reasoning across large, interconnected codebases; and Rakuten is applying it to incident response. OpenAI notes that momentum is spreading beyond engineering as teams use Codex to pull together context from different tools, reason through information, and produce briefs, plans, drafts, and follow-ups.

Codex is also expanding beyond pure coding into browser-based work, image generation, memory, and ongoing cross-application tasks—areas OpenAI frames as "knowledge work" that can benefit every team, not just developers. Organizations interested in bringing a Codex Lab to their team can fill out an interest form or reach their OpenAI contact. The company says it is eager to help more organizations deploy Codex and expects its GSI partners to extend that reach globally.

Why this matters

  • Seven major systems integrators are now formally equipped to deliver Codex at enterprise scale, creating a ready-made adoption channel for large organizations.
  • Developer usage has grown from 3 million to over 4 million weekly in just two weeks, signaling rapid momentum beyond the initial developer audience.
  • Codex is expanding from coding into broader knowledge-work tasks, widening the addressable use case across non-engineering teams within the same organization.
