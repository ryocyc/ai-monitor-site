OpenAI Open-Sources MRC Networking Protocol to Shrink AI Training Failures at Scale

OpenAI has contributed a new networking protocol called MRC (Multipath Reliable Connection) to the Open Compute Project, targeting the reliability bottlenecks that plague large distributed AI training runs.

Article body

OpenAI has released MRC (Multipath Reliable Connection), a networking protocol designed to make large-scale AI training clusters more resilient to hardware failures. The protocol has been contributed to the Open Compute Project (OCP), an industry body that publishes open hardware and infrastructure specs for data centers.

MRC is engineered around multipath communication — routing training workloads across redundant network paths so that a failed link or GPU node does not stall an entire distributed job. In supercomputer-class clusters running thousands of accelerators, even transient network faults can cause costly restarts; MRC aims to absorb those failures at the transport layer without restarting work.

The practical consequence for teams operating or procuring large training infrastructure is a more predictable compute dollar: fewer wasted GPU-hours from network-induced interruptions, and a lower operational burden on the engineers managing the cluster. While OpenAI has not published benchmark numbers or a full technical paper alongside the OCP release, the contribution signals that the company views networking resilience as a critical lever for the next generation of foundation model training.

Why this matters

  • Distributed training clusters with thousands of GPUs are one of the most expensive and failure-prone environments in AI infrastructure; a protocol that reduces restart frequency directly lowers compute cost.
  • Releasing MRC through OCP makes the spec available to hyperscalers, cloud providers, and enterprise AI teams building or operating similar-scale clusters, potentially raising the baseline resilience of the broader training infrastructure ecosystem.
  • OpenAI's move follows a pattern of major AI labs open-sourcing foundational infrastructure components — similar to Meta's contribution of GPU server designs — indicating that hardware-level efficiency is becoming a competitive and collaborative frontier.
