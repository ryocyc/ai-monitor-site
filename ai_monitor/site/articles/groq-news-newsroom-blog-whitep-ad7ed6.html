Article body

On December 24, 2025, Groq announced a non-exclusive inference technology licensing agreement with NVIDIA, framed as a bid to accelerate AI inference at global scale. The arrangement appears to position Groq's LPU (Language Processing Unit) architecture as complementary to NVIDIA's GPU stack rather than in direct competition with it.

A week earlier, on December 18, Groq disclosed a partnership with the U.S. Department of Energy aimed at advancing AI inference and next-generation computing infrastructure. The DOE relationship underscores growing government-level interest in specialized inference hardware as part of national computing strategy.

Earlier in the quarter, Groq opened an Asia-Pacific data center in Sydney (November 17) and entered a partnership with Paytm in India to power real-time AI for payments and platform intelligence (November 5). Both moves reflect an aggressive geographic expansion drive that had already produced a European data center in Helsinki and a sovereign AI deal with Bell Canada earlier in 2025.
