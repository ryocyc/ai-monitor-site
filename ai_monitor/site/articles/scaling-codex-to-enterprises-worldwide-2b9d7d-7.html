Scaling Codex to enterprises worldwide

OpenAI is pairing its new Codex Labs service with a roster of major systems integrators to accelerate adoption across large engineering organizations.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched Codex Labs, a program that embeds OpenAI experts directly into enterprise organizations to help teams integrate Codex into real production workflows through hands-on workshops and working sessions. The company says the goal is to help enterprises move from early usage to repeatable, scaled deployment more quickly. Organizations can apply for a Codex Lab engagement through a form on OpenAI's site or by reaching out to their OpenAI account team.

The company is simultaneously partnering with seven leading global systems integrators (GSIs) — Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services — to meet what OpenAI describes as demand outpacing its own capacity to assist enterprises. These firms will identify high-value Codex use cases across the software development lifecycle and help customers transition from pilots to production-ready deployments. Accenture's Chief AI Officer, Lan Guan, is quoted saying that her professionals are using Codex to move from static requirements to working solutions in hours rather than weeks.

Codex now has more than 4 million weekly active developers, up from 3 million just two weeks prior. On the enterprise side, the post cites Virgin Atlantic using it to increase test coverage and team velocity, Ramp for code review acceleration, Notion for building new features, Cisco for reasoning across large interconnected codebases, and Rakuten for incident response. OpenAI also notes that Codex is expanding beyond coding into browser-based work, image generation, memory, and cross-tool task execution — a shift it says opens the product to non-engineering teams.

Why this matters

  • A growing roster of GSIs means enterprises can now access Codex deployment support through firms already embedded in large IT transformation programs, lowering the barrier to production-scale adoption.
  • The jump from 3 million to 4 million weekly developers in two weeks signals accelerating individual adoption that enterprises are now racing to institutionalize before it fragments into uncontrolled shadow AI.
  • OpenAI's explicit move to position Codex as a cross-team productivity tool — not just a coding assistant — signals a broader ambition to capture knowledge-worker workflows beyond the engineering org.
