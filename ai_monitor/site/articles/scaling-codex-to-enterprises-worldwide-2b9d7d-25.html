OpenAI Launches Codex Labs and GSI Partner Network to Scale Enterprise Adoption

OpenAI is pairing Codex Labs—direct on-site consulting—with a roster of major global systems integrators to accelerate how enterprises deploy the coding tool at scale.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched Codex Labs, a program that dispatches its own experts directly into organizations to help teams integrate Codex into existing workflows and move from early pilots to repeatable production deployments. The company says the goal is straightforward: help enterprises get real value from Codex faster. Beyond Codex Labs, OpenAI is partnering with seven leading global systems integrators—Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services—to widen that reach. These firms will work with enterprise customers to identify high-value Codex use cases across the software development lifecycle and move pilots into production.

The push follows rapid uptake: OpenAI says more than 4 million developers were using Codex weekly as of mid-April, up from 3 million just two weeks prior. Enterprise adoption is also accelerating, with companies like Virgin Atlantic using Codex to boost test coverage and team velocity, Ramp using it for code review, Notion for feature development, Cisco for reasoning across large interconnected repositories, and Rakuten for incident response. OpenAI notes that what often begins with one engineering team expands quickly as leaders observe gains in speed and output.

Accenture's Chief AI Officer, Lan Guan, offered a quote praising the tool's speed: "Our professionals are using Codex to move from static requirements to working solutions in hours, not weeks." OpenAI acknowledges that demand from enterprises is currently outpacing its ability to help them adopt at the pace they'd like, which is part of why the GSI partner network was built. Organizations interested in a Codex Lab can fill out an interest form or contact their OpenAI team.

Why this matters

  • Seven major GSIs—Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and TCS—are now formally enrolled to deploy Codex at enterprise scale, giving OpenAI a global delivery channel beyond its own team.
  • Enterprise use cases are spanning the full software development lifecycle, from Virgin Atlantic's test coverage work to Rakuten's incident response, signaling broader organizational buy-in beyond individual developer adoption.
  • The move into knowledge-work tasks—briefs, plans, checklists, drafts—suggests OpenAI is positioning Codex as a general productivity layer for non-engineering teams, not just a coding tool.
