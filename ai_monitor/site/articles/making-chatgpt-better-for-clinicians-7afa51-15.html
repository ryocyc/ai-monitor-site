OpenAI Makes ChatGPT Free for Verified Clinicians in the U.S.

OpenAI has launched a free version of ChatGPT tailored for clinical work, available at no cost to verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

The new offering, called ChatGPT for Clinicians, is designed to handle tasks such as documentation, medical research, and care consultation. Clinicians in the U.S. who verify their credentials can access it at no charge through ChatGPT, with optional HIPAA support available through a Business Associate Agreement for eligible accounts. OpenAI frames the move as a response to mounting strain on the U.S. healthcare system, where clinicians are juggling rising patient volumes with growing administrative loads and an expanding body of medical literature. The company cites an American Medical Association survey showing physician AI use climbing to 72%, up from 48% the prior year, and says clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past year.

The product draws on OpenAI's current frontier models, including GPT-5.4, and bundles several features aimed at clinical workflows: a trusted search tool pulling from peer-reviewed medical sources, a deep research function for surveying journals, and a skills system that lets clinicians save and replay repeatable tasks like referral letters or prior authorization drafts. It also includes a pathway for evidence review to count toward continuing medical education credits. OpenAI emphasizes that conversations are not used to train models and that multi-factor authentication is available.

Before release, physician advisors tested the system across 6,924 real conversations in clinical care, documentation, and research, rating 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. In an internal evaluation where GPT-5.4 was pitted against human physicians given unlimited time and web access, the model outperformed on the task set. OpenAI is also releasing HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for clinician chat tasks, and a Health Blueprint outlining recommendations for responsible AI integration in U.S. healthcare. The free offering is currently U.S.-only; OpenAI says it plans to expand to additional countries, starting with a pilot through the Better Evidence Network.

Why this matters

  • Millions of clinicians already use general ChatGPT for patient-facing work, but a purpose-built, free tier for credentialed professionals addresses both capability gaps and access equity.
  • The 99.6% safety rating from physician-reviewed test conversations and a benchmark showing GPT-5.4 outperforming human physicians offer concrete, if internal, evidence of model readiness for clinical settings.
  • Optional HIPAA compliance through a BAA bridges a gap that has kept many individual clinicians from using AI tools with real patient information, without requiring an organizational subscription.
