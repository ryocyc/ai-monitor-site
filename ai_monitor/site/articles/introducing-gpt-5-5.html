OpenAI Releases GPT-5.5, Targeting Agentic Coding and Knowledge Work

OpenAI has released GPT-5.5, a model designed to handle complex, multi-step tasks autonomously across coding, research, and computer use—with efficiency gains that the company says do not come at the cost of speed.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-24 04:44 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::introducing-gpt-5-5 Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI on April 23, 2026 launched GPT-5.5, describing it as its most capable model to date for real-world work. According to the company, the model can take on messy, multi-part tasks—writing and debugging code, researching online, building documents and spreadsheets, and operating software—and see them through to completion with less human micromanagement. The focus areas the company highlights are agentic coding, computer use, knowledge work, and early scientific research.

On benchmark figures OpenAI shared publicly, GPT-5.5 scores 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (which tests complex command-line workflows), compared with GPT-5.4's 75.1%. On SWE-Bench Pro, which measures real-world GitHub issue resolution, GPT-5.5 reaches 58.6%. The company also claims the new model matches GPT-5.4 per-token latency in real-world serving while using significantly fewer tokens to complete the same tasks. Early testers—including senior engineers and founders at companies like Every, MagicPath, and Cursor—offered unsolicited praise, with one NVIDIA engineer reportedly saying losing access felt like "having a limb amputated."

GPT-5.5 is rolling out now to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise users in both ChatGPT and Codex. GPT-5.5 Pro is available to Pro, Business, and Enterprise tiers. API access is not yet available; OpenAI says it is working closely with partners on the safety and security requirements before serving the model at scale through the API. The company notes it conducted red team evaluations focused on advanced cybersecurity and biology capabilities and gathered feedback from nearly 200 trusted early-access partners before launch.

Why this matters

  • GPT-5.5 sets new state-of-the-art scores on several external coding benchmarks, representing a clear step up in agentic capability that OpenAI says carries through to real engineering work.
  • The model achieves these gains without a latency penalty, using fewer tokens to complete the same tasks—a meaningful efficiency claim alongside the raw capability jump.
  • OpenAI's internal adoption data shows over 85% of the company uses Codex weekly across software engineering, finance, data science, and other functions, suggesting this model will shape how AI-assisted work actually operates inside large organizations.
