OpenAI Launches Codex Labs to Bring AI Coding Tools to Enterprise Engineering Teams

OpenAI is pairing direct on-the-ground support through Codex Labs with a network of seven global systems integrators to help enterprises move Codex from pilot to production.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI on April 21, 2026 announced Codex Labs, a program that dispatches OpenAI experts directly into organizations to run hands-on workshops and working sessions aimed at integrating Codex into real engineering workflows. The goal, per the company, is to help enterprise teams move from early usage to repeatable, scalable deployment. Organizations can express interest via a form on OpenAI's site or by reaching their OpenAI account team.

To address what OpenAI describes as demand outpacing its own capacity to help enterprises move quickly, the company is simultaneously partnering with seven leading global systems integrators: Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS). These firms are already using Codex internally to reshape how their own teams operate, and they will help enterprise customers identify high-value use cases across the software development lifecycle and carry those from pilot into production. An Accenture representative said the firm is using Codex to move from static requirements to working solutions in hours rather than weeks.

The announcement arrives against a backdrop of rapid adoption growth. OpenAI said that more than 3 million developers were using Codex weekly in early April, and that figure has since climbed to more than 4 million. Early enterprise adopters include Virgin Atlantic, which is using Codex to increase test coverage and team velocity; Ramp, which is applying it to code review; Notion, which is using it to build new features; Cisco, which is using it to reason across large interconnected repositories; and Rakuten, which is using it for incident response. OpenAI also noted that Codex is expanding beyond pure coding into browser-based work, image generation, memory, and task handoffs across tools and apps.

Why this matters

  • A dedicated partner channel gives enterprises a clearer on-ramp to production-scale Codex deployment than individual pilots alone.
  • Seven major GSIs committing to use Codex internally means a consistent deployment playbook is likely to emerge across industries.
  • The jump from 3 million to 4 million weekly developers in roughly two weeks signals accelerating momentum that enterprises will need to evaluate alongside their own adoption strategies.
