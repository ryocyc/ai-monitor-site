OpenAI Launches Free ChatGPT for Verified U.S. Clinicians

OpenAI has released a dedicated version of ChatGPT for verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States, at no cost.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has released ChatGPT for Clinicians, a version of its AI assistant built specifically for tasks like medical documentation and research, and is offering it free to any verified physician, nurse practitioner, physician assistant, or pharmacist in the U.S. The product includes access to frontier AI models for clinical use, reusable workflow "skills" for tasks such as prior authorization letters and patient instructions, and a trusted medical search tool grounded in peer-reviewed sources. Deep research capabilities across medical journals are also built in, and eligible clinicians can earn continuing medical education (CME) credits through evidence review completed within ChatGPT itself.

The launch comes as clinician adoption of AI has surged: a 2026 American Medical Association survey cited by OpenAI shows 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year, with clinician usage of ChatGPT more than doubling over the past year. OpenAI says it worked with hundreds of physician advisors to shape the product and that physician advisors have reviewed over 700,000 model responses to date, with a new response reviewed every few minutes. A pre-release test involved 6,924 conversations across clinical care, documentation, and research; physicians rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. Optional HIPAA support via a Business Associate Agreement is available for eligible accounts, and conversations are not used to train models.

OpenAI also introduced HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark covering three clinician use cases—care consultation, documentation, and medical research—using physician-authored conversations and multi-stage adjudication to assess performance. According to OpenAI, GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperforms base GPT-5.4, other OpenAI and external models, and human physicians on that benchmark. Access is initially limited to verified U.S. clinicians, with plans to expand to additional countries via a Better Evidence Network pilot. A Health Blueprint offering guidance on responsible AI integration in U.S. healthcare was released alongside the announcement.

Why this matters

  • Physician AI use has jumped from 48% to 72% in one year, according to an AMA survey cited by OpenAI.
  • Clinician ChatGPT usage has more than doubled in the past year, signaling rapid adoption of AI in healthcare settings.
  • OpenAI is releasing HealthBench Professional as an open benchmark, allowing the broader medical and AI community to measure and track clinician-facing AI performance over time.
