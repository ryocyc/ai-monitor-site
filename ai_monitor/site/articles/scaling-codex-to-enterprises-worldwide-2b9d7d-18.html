Scaling Codex to enterprises worldwide

OpenAI is launching Codex Labs and partnering with seven global systems integrators to accelerate enterprise adoption of its coding assistant.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has unveiled Codex Labs, a new initiative that puts OpenAI experts directly into organizations to help teams integrate Codex into real workflows through hands-on workshops and working sessions. The goal is to help enterprises move from early adoption to repeatable, production-ready deployments as quickly as possible. The company says demand from enterprises is currently outpacing its ability to serve them alone, prompting the parallel push with external partners.

To scale that reach, OpenAI has also partnered with seven leading global systems integrators: Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS). These firms will identify and deploy high-value Codex use cases across the software development lifecycle, helping customers move from pilots to full production. Several partners, including Accenture, are already using Codex internally to reshape how their own teams operate, in order to build repeatable delivery methods for clients.

Adoption metrics underscore the momentum. OpenAI says more than 3 million developers were using Codex weekly in early April 2026, growing to over 4 million just two weeks later. Early enterprise customers include Virgin Atlantic, which is using Codex to increase test coverage and team velocity; Ramp, which is accelerating code review; Notion, which is building new features faster; Cisco, which is reasoning across large interconnected repositories; and Rakuten, which is applying it to incident response. Notably, Codex is also expanding beyond pure coding to tasks like browser-based work, image generation, memory, and cross-tool workflows that span knowledge work across organizations.

Why this matters

  • OpenAI is now channeling enterprise demand through both internal expertise (Codex Labs) and an established GSI network, a significant structural shift in how it scales beyond individual developers.
  • Seven of the world's largest systems integrators are committing to train their own workforces on Codex and deliver it to clients, which could rapidly accelerate adoption inside Fortune 500-scale organizations.
  • The crossover from pure coding to browser-based work, image generation, and multi-tool knowledge tasks signals OpenAI's intent to position Codex as a broad enterprise productivity layer, not just a developer tool.
