Mistral March 2026 Releases: Voxtral TTS, Mistral Small 4, Leanstral, Moderation 2603

Mistral shipped four distinct model releases in March 2026, spanning text-to-speech, a hybrid instruct-reasoning-coder, a formal proof agent, and an updated moderation system.

Mistral Changelog 2026-04-22 23:49 UTC Key: mistral-changelog::identity::mistral-24.09 Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

On March 23, Mistral released Voxtral TTS (voxtral-tts-2603), a text-to-speech model described as state-of-the-art, featuring zero-shot voice cloning, multilingual support, and real-time streaming. This marks Mistral's entry into the audio generation space, joining the earlier Voxtral Mini family of audio and transcription models. Six days later on March 16, the company released Mistral Small 4 (mistral-small-2603), a hybrid model that unifies instruct, reasoning, and coding capabilities in a single multimodal package with a 256,000-token context window. Also on March 16, Mistral released Leanstral (labs-leanstral-2603), its first open-source code agent built specifically for Lean 4 formal proof engineering — a notably niche tooling area compared to the broader developer tooling in the rest of the release slate. On March 12, the company released Mistral Moderation 2603 (mistral-moderation-2603), alongside an API-level expansion of Custom Guardrails support that allows guardrails to be configured on individual Agents, on POST /v1/conversations, and on POST /v1/chat/completions requests.

Why this matters

  • Voxtral TTS brings Mistral into the competitive text-to-speech market with zero-shot voice cloning, a feature that typically requires much larger vendors.
  • Mistral Small 4's 256k context window is among the largest available in a hybrid instruct-reasoning model, potentially enabling long-document coding and analysis use cases without context truncation.
  • Leanstral targets a narrow but growing academic and formal-methods developer audience interested in Lean 4 proof assistants, an area where open-source tooling is still sparse.
