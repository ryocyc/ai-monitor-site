OpenAI Makes ChatGPT Free for Verified U.S. Clinicians

OpenAI has released a dedicated, no-cost version of ChatGPT for verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI announced on April 22, 2026 that ChatGPT for Clinicians — a version of the AI assistant tailored for medical tasks such as documentation, care consultation, and medical research — is now available for free to verified individual clinicians in the U.S. The offering includes access to OpenAI's frontier models, reusable skills for repeatable clinical workflows, cited answers drawn from peer-reviewed medical sources, and a deep research mode for literature reviews. Eligible clinicians can opt into HIPAA compliance support via a Business Associate Agreement. Conversations are not used to train models, and multi-factor authentication is included.

The launch comes as clinician adoption of AI has surged. According to a 2026 American Medical Association survey cited by OpenAI, 72% of physicians report using AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. The company says clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past year, prompting it to formalize the offering beyond the organizational-level ChatGPT for Healthcare product introduced earlier this year.

Alongside the free clinician tier, OpenAI introduced HealthBench Professional — an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks covering care consult, documentation, and medical research. The benchmark uses physician-authored conversations and multi-stage adjudication. OpenAI says GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperformed base GPT-5.4, other external models, and human physicians on the benchmark, and that physician advisors rated 99.6% of 6,924 tested conversations as safe and accurate. The benchmark dataset and paper are publicly available, and OpenAI published a Health Blueprint outlining recommendations for responsible AI integration in U.S. healthcare.

Why this matters

  • Clinician adoption of AI has reached a tipping point — 72% of U.S. physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from under half a year ago.
  • Free, individual access to a clinically focused ChatGPT with verified medical sourcing could meaningfully reduce administrative burden for frontline clinicians.
  • The public release of HealthBench Professional establishes a transparent benchmark for measuring AI performance on real clinician tasks, enabling ongoing accountability in healthcare AI.
