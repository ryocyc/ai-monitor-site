OpenAI Offers Free ChatGPT to Verified U.S. Clinicians

OpenAI is making a dedicated, free version of ChatGPT available to verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States, with plans to expand to other countries.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI on April 22, 2026 launched ChatGPT for Clinicians, a purpose-built version of its AI assistant tailored for medical documentation, care consultations, and literature research. The offering is free to verified U.S. physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists, and is designed to slot into everyday clinical workflows. The company says it consulted hundreds of physician advisors throughout development and subjected the product to extensive pre-release testing before opening it up.

The product comes with a set of features specifically aimed at clinical environments: access to OpenAI's frontier models for healthcare tasks, reusable "skills" for repeatable workflows like referral letters and prior authorizations, real-time search drawing on peer-reviewed medical sources, automated deep research across medical journals, and an optional HIPAA compliance pathway through a Business Associate Agreement. Clinicians using the platform can also have certain evidence reviews count toward continuing medical education credits. Conversations are not used to train models, and multi-factor authentication is included.

To validate the tool before launch, physician advisors reviewed more than 700,000 model responses reflecting real-world clinical use, with a new response audited every few minutes. They also conducted 6,924 live test conversations across care, documentation, and research, rating 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. In a subset of 355 examples reviewed by three independent physicians, ChatGPT for Clinicians cited the specified sources more often than the human physicians did. OpenAI is also releasing HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark and dataset for measuring AI performance on real clinician chat tasks, along with a Health Blueprint outlining recommendations for responsible AI integration in U.S. healthcare.

Why this matters

  • AI adoption among physicians has surged to 72%, up from 48% last year, according to the AMA's 2026 survey, signaling a rapid shift toward AI-assisted clinical practice.
  • ChatGPT for Clinicians provides free, verified access to frontier AI models with optional HIPAA compliance, removing a key cost and compliance barrier for individual practitioners.
  • The concurrent release of HealthBench Professional and the Health Blueprint establishes OpenAI's public-facing benchmarks and ethical framework for AI in healthcare, aiming to set industry standards.
