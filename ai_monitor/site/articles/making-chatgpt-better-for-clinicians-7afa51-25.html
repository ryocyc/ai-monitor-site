Making ChatGPT better for clinicians

OpenAI is making ChatGPT free for verified physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists in the United States, with a set of features purpose-built for clinical workflows.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI announced on April 22, 2026 that ChatGPT for Clinicians is now available at no cost to verified individual healthcare professionals in the U.S., starting with physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists. The product is built for tasks including documentation, care consultation, and medical research, and includes access to frontier AI models, reusable skills for repeatable workflows like prior authorizations and referral letters, cited answers from peer-reviewed medical sources, and a deep-research function for reviewing medical literature. An optional HIPAA-compliant path through a Business Associate Agreement is also available for eligible accounts.

The release is backed by a broad evaluation program. OpenAI says its physician advisors have reviewed more than 700,000 model responses to date, and that before launch, 6,924 test conversations were run in real clinical settings by physicians, who rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. The company is also publishing HealthBench Professional, an open benchmark for clinician-facing AI tasks across care consultation, documentation, and research. According to OpenAI, GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperforms base GPT-5.4, other OpenAI and external models, and responses produced by human physicians given unbounded time and web access.

Usage data underscores the demand. An American Medical Association survey cited by OpenAI found 72% of physicians now use AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year, and the company says clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled over the past twelve months. Global expansion is planned, beginning with a pilot through the Better Evidence Network for verified clinicians outside the U.S., pending local regulations.

Why this matters

  • A majority of physicians already use AI in clinical practice, and OpenAI is now offering a purpose-built, free tier to individual verified clinicians in the U.S.
  • The company is releasing HealthBench Professional as an open benchmark, allowing the broader healthcare and research community to independently measure AI performance on clinician-level tasks.
  • AI model responses in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperformed human physician responses on the same tasks under controlled conditions, according to OpenAI's own evaluation.
