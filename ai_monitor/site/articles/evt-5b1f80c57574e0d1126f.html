DeepInfra Adds GLM-5.1 to Its Cloud Inference Roster, Expanding Access to Zhipu AI's Agentic Model Family

DeepInfra has made GLM-5.1, Zhipu AI's flagship agentic engineering model, available on its managed inference infrastructure, giving builders a usage-based way to tap the model without self-hosting.

Article body

DeepInfra's pricing and model catalog page now lists GLM-5.1 as available, positioning it as a state-of-the-art agentic engineering model. The addition broadens DeepInfra's coverage of frontier models from Chinese AI labs alongside existing families such as Claude, DeepSeek, Llama, Mistral, and Qwen.

DeepInfra operates a managed inference platform that hosts a wide range of model types — including text generation, automatic speech recognition, embeddings, rerankers, text-to-image, text-to-speech, text-to-video, and zero-shot image classification. The service is priced on a pay-what-you-use basis with no long-term contracts or upfront costs, and supports model families spanning Claude, DeepSeek, Flux, Gemini, Llama, Mistral, Nemotron, and Qwen.

For developers and engineering teams looking to integrate agentic capabilities — tasks that require models to plan, use tools, and maintain context across multi-step workflows — GLM-5.1's availability on a managed, on-demand platform removes the operational burden of self-hosted deployment. This positions DeepInfra as a practical backend option for teams that want API-level access to the model without managing GPU infrastructure.

Why this matters

  • GLM-5.1 is Zhipu AI's leading model family designed for agentic workflows, and making it available on DeepInfra gives teams a managed path to use it without owning or provisioning their own GPU infrastructure.
  • DeepInfra's pay-what-you-use model removes upfront commitment, making it easier for smaller teams or projects to experiment with and integrate agentic AI into production pipelines.
  • The addition reinforces the ongoing trend of third-party cloud providers expanding their model catalogs to include frontier models from Chinese AI labs, broadening the competitive landscape for managed inference.
