AWS Security Agent and DevOps Agent Exit Preview, Enter General Availability

Amazon has made its autonomous frontier agents — the AWS Security Agent for on-demand penetration testing and the AWS DevOps Agent — generally available, signaling a major step toward AI-driven cloud security and operations at scale.

Article body

Amazon Web Services has officially moved two of its flagship autonomous AI systems out of preview and into general availability. The AWS Security Agent, which provides on-demand penetration testing capabilities, and the AWS DevOps Agent are now broadly accessible to customers and partners, the company announced on 31 March 2026. Both agents fall under AWS's newly defined category of "frontier agents," a class of AI systems capable of working independently to achieve complex, long-running goals without continuous human oversight.

These frontier agents are designed to operate persistently for hours or even days, scaling concurrently across multiple tasks to handle security testing and cloud operations at a scale previously impractical for human-led teams. AWS previewed the capabilities as part of its re:Invent announcements, where early customers and partners reported that the Security Agent was able to comprehensively handle penetration testing workflows autonomously.

The release marks a deliberate shift in how AWS positions AI within enterprise cloud environments — not as a point-in-time assistant, but as a persistent, autonomous actor capable of carrying out extended security and operational workflows. Together, the two agents aim to change the way organizations both secure and operate their software on AWS infrastructure.

Why this matters

  • Organizations can now automate penetration testing on demand, potentially reducing the cost and turnaround time of routine security assessments without relying solely on external consultants.
  • DevOps teams gain an AI agent capable of handling extended, multi-step cloud operations autonomously, freeing engineers from repetitive operational tasks and enabling them to focus on higher-value work.
  • The general availability signals that AWS views autonomous, long-running AI agents as a core product category — not an experimental feature — signaling a broader industry shift toward AI systems that act independently over hours or days rather than responding to single prompts.
