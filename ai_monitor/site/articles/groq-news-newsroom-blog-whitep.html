Article body

Groq entered a non-exclusive inference technology licensing agreement with NVIDIA, announced December 24, 2025, covering AI inference at global scale. The agreement is positioned as a mechanism for accelerating inference deployment internationally.

The company simultaneously deepened ties with the U.S. Department of Energy to advance AI inference and next-generation computing infrastructure, per a December 18, 2025 entry. Separately, Groq launched a Sydney data center in November 2025 to expand its Asia-Pacific footprint, targeting real-time inference demand in the region.

Groq also extended its reach into the Indian market through a partnership with Paytm, focused on delivering real-time AI for payments and platform intelligence, announced November 5, 2025. Further partnerships include collaborations with HUMAIN One (enterprise AI operating system), Aljammaz Technologies (MENA region), and McLaren Racing (Formula 1).

Why this matters

  • The non-exclusive NVIDIA licensing agreement signals that Groq's inference hardware is being recognized as a viable global-scale solution, even alongside NVIDIA's own GPU stack.
  • The Department of Energy partnership places Groq at the center of U.S. government-backed AI infrastructure planning, a significant endorsement for a relatively young company.
  • The Sydney data center gives Groq its first dedicated Asia-Pacific presence, directly addressing latency-sensitive inference workloads in a region with growing AI demand.
