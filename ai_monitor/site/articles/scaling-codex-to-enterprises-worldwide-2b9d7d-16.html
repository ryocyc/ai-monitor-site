Scaling Codex to enterprises worldwide

OpenAI is pairing its new Codex Labs program with a roster of global systems integrators to help enterprises move from pilot use of its coding tool to full production deployments.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-22 12:43 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI has launched Codex Labs, a direct-engagement program that places OpenAI experts inside enterprise organizations to help teams integrate Codex into real engineering workflows. The company says the goal is to accelerate time-to-value from initial use to repeatable, scaled deployment. Hands-on workshops and working sessions form the core of the Labs offering, with OpenAI positioning it as a bridge between early adoption and operational roll-out.

The demand from enterprise customers is outpacing OpenAI's capacity to help on its own, so the company is also partnering with seven global systems integrators—Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS)—to extend that impact. These firms will help enterprise clients identify high-value Codex use cases, move from pilots to production, and embed the tool across the software development lifecycle. All seven partners are also using Codex internally to build repeatable delivery methods for their own clients.

Codex, which now has more than 4 million weekly active developers (up from 3 million in early April), is finding traction across a range of named enterprise users: Virgin Atlantic is using it to increase test coverage and team velocity; Ramp is using it to speed code review; Notion is building new features with it; Cisco is applying it to large interconnected codebases; and Rakuten is using it for incident response. Beyond pure coding, OpenAI says teams are also using Codex for browser-based work, image generation, memory, and knowledge-work tasks like drafting briefs, plans, and follow-ups—pointing to a broader expansion of the tool's scope beyond engineering teams.

Why this matters

  • Seven major GSIs including Accenture, PwC, and TCS are now official Codex deployment partners, giving OpenAI an enterprise delivery channel it did not previously have.
  • Codex's developer base grew from 3 million to 4 million weekly active users in roughly two weeks, indicating a rapid acceleration in adoption.
  • OpenAI is explicitly positioning Codex as a tool for non-engineering teams as well, signalling a push to expand the product's addressable market beyond coding.

  • The article was published on OpenAI's newsroom on April 21, 2026. It includes named enterprise customers, a specific list of seven GSI partners, and quantified developer growth figures, making the claims checkable. No pricing, contract terms, or regional availability details were included.
