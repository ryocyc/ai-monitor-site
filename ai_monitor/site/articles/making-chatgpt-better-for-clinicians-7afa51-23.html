ChatGPT for Clinicians: Free Access for Verified Physicians, NPs, PAs, and Pharmacists

OpenAI has launched a free, clinician-focused version of ChatGPT for verified U.S. physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and pharmacists, with optional HIPAA compliance.

OpenAI Newsroom 2026-04-23 07:07 UTC Key: openai-newsroom::identity::making-chatgpt-better-for-clinicians Confidence: strong Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI on April 22, 2026 unveiled ChatGPT for Clinicians, a dedicated version of its AI tool tailored for medical documentation, care consultations, and medical research. The product is free for any verified U.S. physician, NP, PA, or pharmacist at launch, with plans to expand internationally through a partnership with the Better Evidence Network. Optional HIPAA compliance is available via a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) for eligible accounts.

The launch is backed by a surge in clinician AI adoption. According to an American Medical Association survey, 72% of physicians now report using AI in clinical practice, up from 48% the prior year. OpenAI says clinician usage of ChatGPT has more than doubled in the past year, prompting the company to formalize a health-specific product. ChatGPT for Clinicians includes frontier model access, reusable Skills for tasks like prior authorizations and referral letters, cited answers from peer-reviewed sources, and a CME credit feature that automatically tracks eligible evidence reviews.

OpenAI introduced HealthBench Professional alongside the product, an open benchmark for real clinician chat tasks. In testing, 6,924 conversations were evaluated by physician advisors across clinical care, documentation, and research, with 99.6% of responses rated as safe and accurate. On a curated subset of 355 cases with physician-authored ground-truth citations, GPT-5.4 in the ChatGPT for Clinicians workspace outperformed base GPT-5.4, all other OpenAI and external models, and human physicians given unbounded time and web access. OpenAI also released a Health Blueprint outlining recommendations for responsible AI integration in U.S. healthcare.

Why this matters

  • Free, verified clinician access removes a cost barrier for individual practitioners who want AI support but lack enterprise procurement options.
  • The launch formalizes what OpenAI describes as a doubling of clinician ChatGPT usage over the past year, signaling AI is taking hold in routine clinical workflows.
  • HealthBench Professional provides an open, physician-reviewed benchmark that lets the broader medical and AI community track performance and safety progress.
