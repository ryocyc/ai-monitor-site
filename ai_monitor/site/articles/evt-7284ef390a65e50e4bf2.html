ChatGPT's New Trusted Contact Feature Can Alert a Close Contact if Safety Concerns Are Detected

OpenAI has added an optional Trusted Contact feature to ChatGPT that can notify a designated person when the system detects serious self-harm concerns during a conversation.

OpenAI Newsroom <span data-utc="2026-05-07T18:05:36+00:00">2026-05-07 18:05 UTC</span> Key: evt-7284ef390a65e50e4bf2 Confidence: moderate Mode: claude

Article body

OpenAI on Thursday introduced Trusted Contact, a new optional safety feature within ChatGPT that alerts a user-designated close contact when the system detects serious self-harm concerns during a chat session. The feature is opt-in and gives users control over who is notified and under what circumstances. The announcement was published on the OpenAI Newsroom.

When ChatGPT's safety classifiers identify signals consistent with self-harm risk, Trusted Contact sends a configurable message to the selected individual, which may include relevant context from the conversation to help them respond appropriately. Users can add, remove, or update their trusted contact at any time through ChatGPT settings. The feature joins a broader set of safety interventions OpenAI has layered into ChatGPT over the past year.

For users who opt in, the feature provides a real-world safety net that extends beyond the chatbot itself — connecting AI-generated safety signals to human support networks. For builders and platforms evaluating similar interventions, it represents a concrete example of integrating classifier outputs into actionable outside-the-app workflows.

Why this matters

  • It is one of the first times a major consumer AI product has built a live, user-controlled bridge from safety classifiers to real-world human responders.
  • The feature puts agency in the user's hands: contact selection and notification preferences are user-managed and can be changed at any time.
  • For the AI safety field, it signals a shift from in-app warnings or resource links toward proactive, consent-based outreach to a user's own chosen network.
